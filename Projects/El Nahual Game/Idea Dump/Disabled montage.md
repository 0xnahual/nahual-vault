## **🐉 The Disabled Montage (Reforged Arc)**

- **Opening scene**:
    
    Dark hospital room. He sits in the wheelchair, staring at the word _“disabled”_ stamped on his discharge papers. His jaw clenches.
    
- **The Break**:
    
    One night, rage boiling, he **throws himself out of the chair.** He crashes to the floor. Crawling. Blood on his hands.
    
    He ties a splintered **wooden stick** to his burned leg with rope. The pain is blinding — he screams, but doesn’t stop.
    
- **Montage begins (guitar riff kicks in):**
    
    - Crawling every day, dragging himself until calluses form on his elbows.
        
    - Failing, collapsing, crying — then dragging again.
        
    - Training the one flesh arm until veins pop. Dumbbells, pull-ups, push-ups, sweat burning into scar tissue.
        
    - **Engineering textbooks open** on the desk. He studies prosthetic design at night, scars illuminated by a desk lamp.
        
    - Every time someone says “disabled,” it cuts to him pushing harder — crawling, lifting, sketching blueprints, welding sparks flying.
        
    
- **Milestone scenes:**
    
    - He limps across parallel bars for the first time — collapses, then drags himself up again.
        
    - Prosthetic prototype burns out mid-squat — he rebuilds it stronger.
        
    - Blood on the gym floor, but he stands for a full second unaided.
        
    
- **Climax:**
    
    After months, he straps into a new mechanical leg he forged himself. He takes a single step… then another.
    
    Cuts to the robotic arm attaching — sparks, pain, then he clenches the fist.
    
- **Final shot:**
    
    He stands in front of the mirror. No wheelchair. No pity. Just scars glowing under neon light, metal fused to flesh.
    
    Guitar solo peaks. He mutters under his breath:
    
    > “Disabled? …No. Reforged.”
