## Why This Works

### The clean split: Real World vs Fictional World

Everything in the vault orbits two centers:

1. **Your body, your voice, your transformation** — IXNAH, El Templo, NΛHUΛL music, training, philosophy. This is the REAL world. Your 127kg → 89kg arc. Your postures. Your songs. Your breathing spells. Your system.

2. **The myth you're building** — William's story, El Vacío, the 9 corrupted pillars, the Toltec-inspired universe, cinematic electronic rock. This is the FICTIONAL world. It's autobiography encoded as myth, but it's presented as art, not as personal story.

Before this restructuring, the fictional world was split between "El Nahual" (game) and "Ixtlan" (music) — two names for one universe. Merging them under **Ixtlan Project** makes the fictional world one coherent brand. The game IS Ixtlan. The soundtrack IS Ixtlan. The lore IS Ixtlan. The animations ARE Ixtlan. One name, one universe, one brand.

And the word "nahual" is now reserved for the REAL world: @0xnahual (you), NΛHUΛL (your voice), IXNAH (your body), Nahualismo (your philosophy). Personal. Embodied. True.

### NΛHUΛL as the music artist name

Your personal folk songs are the most emotionally authentic thing in the vault. "De chiquito" is not a Ricardo Bustos song — it's something deeper. NΛHUΛL captures the spirit behind the music without being your legal name. It's intimate but mythological. Mexican but universal. Pronounceable but visually distinct (the Λ characters).

Releasing as "Ricardo Bustos" makes it a diary entry from a person nobody knows. Releasing as "NΛHUΛL" makes it a voice emerging from a world. The world already exists in your vault — the 9 Pillars, the transformation arc, the philosophy. The music is the transmission medium of that world. NΛHUΛL is the transmitter.

Your Spotify artist page for NΛHUΛL can have a bio that says "Ricardo Bustos. Mexico." One sentence connects the mythological to the real. The listener gets both.

### Ixtlan Project as the creative universe

Your vault's Ixtlan "About.md" already describes it as "an artistic movement — music + storytelling + powerful experiences." It was never just a music project. The game bible uses Ixtlan's 9 Pillars as its boss framework. The electronic tracks ("Fearless," "Limitless," "Spirits Never Die") were always meant to be the game's soundtrack. The music and the game were never separate projects — you were treating them as separate because they had different names.

Under **Ixtlan Project**, everything fictional lives in one place:
- The Spotify artist page releases both standalone tracks and game soundtrack
- The YouTube presence holds music videos, game trailers, animation clips, and lore fragments
- The itch.io page hosts the playable game
- Social media lore posts build the world between releases

When someone discovers the Ixtlan Project soundtrack on Spotify and then finds the game — that's not confusing, that's world-building. The music made them curious about the universe. The game lets them enter it.

### @0xnahual is ONE handle for everything

One social media account per platform. Not @ricardobustos for personal and @ixtlanproject for the universe and @eltemplo for the app. One account. Everything flows through it.

Why:
- You're one person. The audience should see one person.
- Multiple accounts divide your attention and your audience.
- The CODE-SWITCHING is the brand. Folk music one day, training clip the next, Ixtlan Project lore on the weekend. That range IS the Nahual identity — shapeshifter, multiple expressions, one source.
- Your bilingual nature IS the brand. Spanish song, English dev thought, Spanglish reflection. Stop trying to segment it. Be the bridge.

The only separation is on streaming platforms (NΛHUΛL for folk, Ixtlan Project for electronic/cinematic). Social media is unified.

**What goes here:** Visual content. The grid is the gallery.

**Stories:** Use for the raw stuff. A 10-second clip of bag work. A 5-second clip of the guitar. A screenshot of the app. A game dev screenshot. Ephemeral, not permanent. This is where the "overflow" lives.

### TikTok: @0xnahual

**What goes here:** Short training clips. This is the discovery engine.

| Content Type | Format |
|-------------|--------|
| Stance holds with timer | 30-60 sec, full body, face visible but not featured |
| Calisthenics moments | 15-30 sec, PR attempts, struggle |
| Judo highlights | 15-30 sec, throws, getting thrown |
| NΛHUΛL song clips | 15-30 sec, vocal + guitar, lyrics on screen |
| Heavy bag | 30-60 sec, raw footage, "caught training" format |

**The strategy:** No talking. No hooks. No "watch till the end." Just a person training, caught on film. Text overlay if context is needed. Timer on screen for stances. That's it.

This is where the "present but not performing" format lives most naturally. TikTok's algorithm rewards authenticity. A raw clip of you holding Ma Bu with shaking legs and a visible timer performs better than a produced fitness video because it feels private — like seeing something you weren't supposed to.

**Language:** Spanish text overlays. The Spanish-language fitness/martial arts niche on TikTok is massively underserved. Own it.

**Ixtlan Project on TikTok:** Not primary here. The occasional 15-second game clip or music snippet, but TikTok is the training platform. Ixtlan Project lives primarily on YouTube and Spotify.

It's the Nahual — the shapeshifter.

**YouTube playlists do the organizing.** Create playlists: "IXNAH Training," "NΛHUΛL Music," "Ixtlan Project," "Philosophy." The channel is one feed, but playlists let people find their thread.

### Spotify

**TWO artist pages. Non-negotiable.**

**Artist 1: NΛHUΛL**
- Genre: Folk / Singer-Songwriter / Latin Alternative
- Language: Spanish
- Vibe: Intimate, vulnerable, guitar, voice, Mexican
- Songs: "De chiquito," "Yo No Me Fui," future personal songs
- Artist bio: "Ricardo Bustos. Mexico. Canciones que se cayeron del silencio."
- Profile image: Something that isn't your face. Hands on guitar. A silhouette. A nahual symbol.
- Release pace: One song every 1-2 months. No campaign. Just appears.

**Artist 2: Ixtlan Project**
- Genre: Electronic Rock / Cinematic / Alternative / Soundtrack
- Language: Mixed (Spanish + English)
- Vibe: Cinematic, aggressive, world-building, Muse-energy
- Songs: "Fearless," "Limitless," "Spirits Never Die," future soundtrack pieces
- Artist bio: "The soundtrack to a world being built. Game. Music. Myth. By @0xnahual."
- Profile image: Game art, symbol, abstract — not a person
- Release pace: One track every 2-3 months. Each release paired with one lore fragment on social media.
- Status: Paused until collaborator appears or game needs a specific track.

**Why two pages:**
- Spotify's algorithm recommends based on listening patterns. Folk listeners and electronic rock listeners are different populations. One artist page with both genres confuses the algorithm and hurts both.
- DistroKid allows multiple artist profiles under one account ($22/year for basic, $36/year for unlimited artists). Two profiles costs nothing extra.
- A NΛHUΛL fan can discover Ixtlan Project through the @0xnahual social links. But they don't get cinematic electronic rock interrupting their folk playlist. Clean.

## What You Do NOT Need

- **A website.** Not yet. App Store listing, Spotify pages, Twitter bio, and Amazon author page are your "website" for now. A real website is a Phase 3 thing.
- **A newsletter.** Not yet. You have no audience to email. Build it through the app first.
- **Separate social accounts per project.** One account. The mix IS the brand.
- **A separate Ixtlan Project social account.** Everything goes through @0xnahual. The Ixtlan Project brand lives on Spotify (artist page), itch.io (game page), and YouTube (playlists). Not on a separate Instagram or Twitter.
- **A content calendar.** Your vault says "overflow only." Trust it.
- **A "launch."** No announcement post. No "I'm back" thread. Just start posting as if you've always been here.
- **A Discord.** Not until you have 1,000+ app users or the Ixtlan Project game has a community.
- **Merch.** Not until the brand means something to other people.
