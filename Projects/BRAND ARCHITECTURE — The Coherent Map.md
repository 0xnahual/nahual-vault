## Why This Works

### NΛHUΛL as the music artist name

Your personal folk songs are the most emotionally authentic thing in the vault. "De chiquito" is not a Ricardo Bustos song — it's something deeper. NΛHUΛL captures the spirit behind the music without being your legal name. It's intimate but mythological. Mexican but universal. Pronounceable but visually distinct (the Λ characters).

Releasing as "Ricardo Bustos" makes it a diary entry from a person nobody knows. Releasing as "NΛHUΛL" makes it a voice emerging from a world. The world already exists in your vault — the 9 Pillars, the transformation arc, the philosophy. The music is the transmission medium of that world. NΛHUΛL is the transmitter.

And here's the practical beauty: your Spotify artist page for NΛHUΛL can have a bio that says "Ricardo Bustos. Mexico." One sentence connects the mythological to the real. The listener gets both.

### IXTLAN stays separate

Your own Music Strategy document says it: "Stop trying to make them the same project. They're not." NΛHUΛL (personal folk) and Ixtlan (electronic rock) are different energies, different genres, different audiences. They need separate Spotify artist pages. Separate visual identities. Separate release timelines.

This is standard in music. Gorillaz and Blur are the same person. Childish Gambino and Donald Glover are the same person. The separation isn't dishonest — it's clarity. Folk song listeners don't want electronic rock in their Discover Weekly. Electronic rock listeners don't want intimate guitar ballads. Separate artist pages let each find its audience without confusing either.

The connection between them lives in @0xnahual, where both get shared. A follower who digs deep sees the same person behind both. But they don't have to — the music stands alone in each identity.

### @0xnahual is ONE handle for everything

One social media account per platform. Not @ricardobustos for personal and @ixtlanproject for music and @eltemplo for the app. One account. Everything flows through it.

Why:
- You're one person. The audience should see one person.
- Multiple accounts divide your attention and your audience.
- The CODE-SWITCHING is the brand. Folk music one day, training clip the next, philosophy the day after, game lore on the weekend. That range IS the Nahual identity — shapeshifter, multiple expressions, one source.
- Your bilingual nature IS the brand. Spanish song, English dev thought, Spanglish reflection. Stop trying to segment it. Be the bridge.

The only separation is on streaming platforms (NΛHUΛL for folk, Ixtlan for electronic). Social media is unified.

**What goes here:** Visual content. The grid is the gallery.

| Content Type | Format | Frequency |

### TikTok: @0xnahual

**What goes here:** Short training clips. This is the discovery engine.

| Content Type | Format |
|-------------|--------|
| Stance holds with timer | 30-60 sec, full body, face visible but not featured |
| Calisthenics moments | 15-30 sec, PR attempts, struggle |
| Judo highlights | 15-30 sec, throws, getting thrown |
| Song clips | 15-30 sec, vocal + guitar, lyrics on screen |
| Heavy bag | 30-60 sec, raw footage, "caught training" format |

**The strategy:** No talking. No hooks. No "watch till the end." Just a person training, caught on film. Text overlay if context is needed. Timer on screen for stances. That's it.

This is where the "present but not performing" format lives most naturally. TikTok's algorithm rewards authenticity. A raw clip of you holding Ma Bu with shaking legs and a visible timer performs better than a produced fitness video because it feels private — like seeing something you weren't supposed to.

**Language:** Spanish text overlays. The Spanish-language fitness/martial arts niche on TikTok is massively underserved. Own it.

## What You Do NOT Need

- **A website.** Not yet. The App Store listing, Spotify artist page, Twitter bio, and Amazon author page are your "website" for now. A real website is a Phase 3 thing.
- **A newsletter.** Not yet. You have no audience to email. Build it through the app first (push notifications > email for a training app).
- **Separate social accounts per project.** One account. The mix IS the brand.
- **A content calendar.** Your vault says "overflow only." Trust it. The content strategy documents in your vault are the weakest writing there because they're someone trying to convince themselves to do something they don't want to do.
- **A "launch."** No announcement post. No "I'm back" thread. No reintroduction. Just start posting as if you've always been here. The first post is whatever is true that day.
- **A Discord.** Not until you have 1,000+ app users. Community infrastructure before community is a ghost town.
- **Merch.** Not until the brand means something to other people.

## The Name Collision — Why It's Fine

You're worried: the game is called El Nahual, the music artist is NΛHUΛL, the handle is 0xnahual, the system is IXNAH (Nahual backwards). Is this confusing?

No. It's a constellation. All the stars orbit the same center. The word "nahual" is the thread.

- **0xnahual** — the programmer identity (0x = hex prefix, engineer's mark)
- **NΛHUΛL** — the artistic identity (Λ characters = visual distinction, mythological weight)
- **El Nahual** — the game (the shapeshifter as character, the mythology made playable)
- **IXNAH** — the system (Nahual reversed = inward journey, the body path)
- **Nahualismo** — the philosophy (the -ismo = the doctrine)

A stranger encountering any of these would eventually discover the others. The repetition isn't confusing — it's a signal. "This person's entire world orbits one idea." That's not scattered. That's focused.

The only thing that would be confusing is if NΛHUΛL (the folk artist) and El Nahual (the game) released something on the same day with no context. Solution: they won't. The game is years away. The music is now. By the time El Nahual releases, NΛHUΛL will be an established artist name and the distinction will be obvious.
