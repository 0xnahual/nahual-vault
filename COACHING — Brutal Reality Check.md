## Part 5: The Risks That Could End This

Being honest about what could go wrong:

### 1. You never ship and the project stays in the vault forever
**Probability: 30%.**

This is the most likely failure mode. Not injury. Not financial collapse. Just... never pressing publish. The vault gets bigger. The outlines get more detailed. The systems get more sophisticated. And nobody ever sees any of it. You retire at 65 having trained every single day for 35 years and written the most complete martial arts philosophy that nobody ever read.

This is the one to watch. Every other risk is external. This one is internal.

### 2. Injury from high training volume
**Probability: 20%.**

11 sessions/week with live martial arts is real volume. A blown knee at Judo. A concussion at K1. A shoulder injury from pullups. Any of these could derail 6-12 months of physical progress. The trigger system helps, but injuries don't always announce themselves.

Mitigation: The trigger system exists. Listen to it. Drop volume at the first sign, not the third.

### 3. Job loss or income disruption
**Probability: 15%.**

AI industry is volatile. Companies restructure. Roles get automated. $8,100/month disappears and suddenly the emergency fund matters — except you don't have one yet.

Mitigation: Build the emergency fund NOW. That's the whole point of La Forja's financial pillar.

### 4. Lifestyle inflation kills the financial foundation
**Probability: 25%.**

$8,100 in Mexico is a lot of money. It's easy to upgrade the apartment, buy gear, eat out more, subscribe to everything — and suddenly you're living paycheck to paycheck at 140,000 MXN/month instead of 15,000/month. The lifestyle inflates to match the income and the emergency fund never materializes.

Mitigation: Track expenses. Restore the financial tracking that died in June.

### 5. Burnout from the schedule
**Probability: 15%.**

You say training isn't grinding. That's true NOW. But 11 sessions/week + 40hr job + creative blocks + Mandarin study + life — sustained for 12-18 months without a real break? Cumulative fatigue is real. It doesn't feel like burnout until it does. And when it hits, it hits everything at once.

Mitigation: The monthly check system. And honest self-assessment. "I enjoy training" today doesn't mean "I enjoy training" in month 14.

### 6. The project is too niche to find an audience
**Probability: 20%.**

Toltec philosophy + Chinese internal arts + K1 + Judo + electronic music + folk music + an app + a book. This is deeply original — but "original" sometimes means "nobody's looking for it." The Venn diagram of people interested in ALL of these things might be very small.

Counter-argument: You don't need ALL the audience to care about ALL the things. Different people enter through different doors. Some come for the transformation. Some for the music. Some for the martial arts. Some for the philosophy. The @0xnahual brand is the hub that connects them. But each spoke needs to be strong enough to attract its own audience.

Mitigation: Don't try to explain the entire universe at once. Lead with ONE thing. Probably the transformation story. It's the most universally compelling. Let people discover the rest.
