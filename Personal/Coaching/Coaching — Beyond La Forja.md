# COACHING — Beyond La Forja

## What Comes Out of the Fire

*Created: March 1, 2026*

## The Man Who Walks Out of La Forja

**March 2027.** Maybe June. Doesn't matter. The conditions gate is met.

Pliegues confirmed: 14.5% body fat. 90 kg. 77 kg of lean mass.

The mirror shows someone that the 127 kg version of you would not recognize. Not just thinner — rebuilt. Shoulders, back, legs built from two years of calisthenics, [[IXNAH Strength — The 7 Compounds|kettlebell swings]], and daily movement. A body that can do pistol squats, loaded [[IXNAH Strength — The 7 Compounds|pullups]], hold [[IXNAH Mobility — Strength at End Range|Ma Bu]] for minutes, throw a person in Judo, take a K1 combination to the body and keep moving.

Emergency fund: 6 months. The $500-to-your-name fear is a memory, not a possibility.

Studio: functional. Equipment set up. Ableton running. The room works.

And somewhere in those 12-18 months, almost without noticing, you shipped something. A post. A clip. An essay. A song. Small. Imperfect. But real. The seal broke.

[[Coaching — Final Training Schedule|La Forja]] is done. The blade comes out of the fire.

## LA REVELACIÓN

### The Phase Where the World Sees What You Built

**Duration: ~12 months. Roughly mid-2027 through mid-2028.**

La Forja was private. Silence. Scaffolding. Internal construction.

La Revelación is when you open the doors.

---

### Month 1-2: The Drop

**The transformation content.**

You have the 127 kg video. Smoking weed. Singing narcocorridos at 237 pounds. You have the Ma Bu video — 90 kg, bald, taking punches with a straight face. And now you have the final form: 14.5%, documented PRs, two martial arts, the [[What is IXNAH|IXNAH]] morning practice on camera.

This isn't a before/after Instagram post. This is a documentary piece. 3-5 minutes. Raw. Real. The voiceover tells the story — not the weight loss story, the consciousness story. "I was asleep. I woke up. This is what the waking up looked like."

@0xnahual goes from 0 posts to the most honest transformation content on the internet. Not because of the physique. Because of the depth. Every other transformation video is "I ate chicken and rice and hit the gym." Yours is: "I built a martial arts system based on Toltec philosophy, studied Chinese internal arts every morning, trained Judo and K1 every evening, and rebuilt myself from the inside out."

Nobody has that story. Nobody.

**Simultaneously:** The Ixtlan Project drops its first track. Electronic. Dark. The Castaneda world in sound. It doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to exist. The music and the transformation content land at the same time. The audience doesn't find a fitness page. They find a universe.

---

### Month 3-6: The System Goes Public

**[[What is IXNAH|IXNAH]] becomes teachable.**

Not an app. Not yet. Start with content.

The 6 domains explained in short videos. You demonstrating the Medicine Postures. The Breathing Spells. The stance transitions — the silk reeling, the hip-leading, the Qi Gong flow. Real training clips from Judo randori and K1 sparring cut against the morning internal practice.

The contrast IS the teaching: "Here's the same man doing silk reeling at sunrise and getting thrown at Judo at night. The internal feeds the external. The external tests the internal. That's IXNAH."

Write the first essays. Not the whole book — pieces of it. The philosophy in digestible form. Post them. Let people argue. Let people question. The friction creates reach.

**[[Kung Fu Temple App Spec|El Templo]] opens to beta.** 10-20 users. Martial artists, friends, people from the content audience. The app becomes a product, not a personal tool. Feedback comes in. The app improves. The training data from real users starts shaping the system.

**NΛHUΛL folk recordings.** The other side. Guitar. Voice. Mexican folk filtered through everything you've become. This isn't the Ixtlan Project electronic darkness — this is the personal, acoustic, soul-level expression. Released quietly. For the people who go deeper than the surface.

---

### Month 6-9: The Audience Finds You

You don't chase the audience. The content accumulates. The algorithm works on volume and consistency, not virality.

By now @0xnahual has:
- The transformation documentary
- Weekly [[What is IXNAH|IXNAH]] training content
- Ixtlan Project music (2-3 tracks minimum)
- NΛHUΛL folk recordings
- Written essays on martial arts philosophy
- A working app that other people use

Some of it hits. Most of it doesn't. Doesn't matter. The body of work exists. Anyone who finds one piece can fall into the rest. The brand architecture you built in silence — the fact that @0xnahual is ONE handle, that IXNAH and Ixtlan Project and NΛHUΛL and El Templo are all connected — that pays off now. One person finds a song. They click the profile. They find a martial arts system. They find a transformation story. They find an app. They find philosophy.

"Wait, this is all one person?"

That's the reaction. That's the brand.

---

### Month 9-12: The Book Takes Shape

The essays you've been posting are chapters in disguise. You realize this around month 9. The structure of the book reveals itself through the writing you've already done.

The 24-chapter outline from 2026 evolves. Some chapters collapse. New ones emerge. The book isn't theoretical anymore — it's grounded in 3+ years of real practice, real fighting, real transformation, documented and shared publicly. Every claim in the book has a video to back it up.

You start writing for real. Not outlines. Not notes. Pages. Chapters. The voice is there — it's been developing through every essay, every post caption, every piece of content. The writing muscle built quietly during La Revelación.

The book doesn't need to be finished by the end of this phase. But it needs to be IN PROGRESS for real. Half-drafted. Alive. Not a vault outline — a living manuscript.

## LA EXPANSIÓN

### The Phase Where You Go to the Source

**Starts: ~Mid-2028. Open-ended.**

---

### China

You've earned it.

Conversational Mandarin. 3+ years of [[What is IXNAH|IXNAH]] practice including Qi Gong and silk reeling. 3 years of Judo, 2 years of K1. A body at 14.5% that can handle intensive training. Emergency fund that covers months of travel. An audience that will follow the journey. An app that can run while you're gone (or you work on it remotely). A remote job that might allow a sabbatical — or enough savings that you can take unpaid time.

**Wudang or Shaolin. 3-6 months.**

Not as a tourist. Not on a 2-week retreat with other Westerners doing Tai Chi in matching uniforms. As a student. Waking up at 5am. Training in Chinese. Learning from lineage holders. Going deep into the internal arts you've been practicing alone for 3 years.

You document everything. Not for content — for the book. For IXNAH. For the integration. You're not there to become a Kung Fu master. You're there to understand the roots of what you've been building, directly from the source.

**What China gives you that nothing else can:**

- Legitimacy. You studied at the source. Not required, but undeniable.
- Depth. Things you can't learn from YouTube or books. Corrections to your Qi Gong. Nuances of silk reeling. Internal arts principles that only transmit teacher-to-student.
- The book's missing chapter. "I built the system, then went to where it came from. Here's what I found. Here's what changed."
- Content that nobody else has. A Mexican martial artist who speaks Mandarin training at Wudang and connecting it to Toltec philosophy. That's not a story that exists anywhere.
- Personal transformation. Immersion in another culture, another language, another training environment. You come back different. The system evolves.

---

### After China

You return. The [[What is IXNAH|IXNAH]] system is complete — not because you added Chinese forms, but because you understood the roots deeply enough to know what your system IS and what it ISN'T. The synthesis is clear:

**[[What is IXNAH|IXNAH]] is not Kung Fu. It's not Judo. It's not K1. It's not Castaneda.**

It's a system that uses the body as the vehicle for consciousness, drawing from Chinese internal arts, live combat training, Toltec philosophy, and modern calisthenics. Built by a man who was dying at 127 kg and rebuilt himself into a martial artist, musician, and philosopher.

That's the book. That's the pitch. That's the brand. And it's TRUE.
