## PART I: ON FINDING A PARTNER

### 1.1 The Real Diagnosis

Your vault has documented the partner question across dozens of files. The conclusion is always the same: you haven't found her. You've made frameworks. You've prayed. You've done active imagination dialogues where your subconscious tells you exactly what's happening.

Your subconscious was right:

> "La parte que te bloquea de tener una pareja es la parte que está herida."

That's the diagnosis. Not your standards. Not your situation. The wound.

The wound has a specific shape:

- **Barbara.** You let her go. She matched your "soul level" — your own words. There's unfinished emotional business there quietly blocking the door to new connections.
- **Naya.** Same pattern — you let go before it could hurt you.
- **The crypto collapse.** Your social world imploded. Your best friend left. The people who knew you turned on you. So you started leaving first — before anyone could do it again. Every relationship since has gotten filtered through that reflex.
- **Isolation built into identity.** You took the pain of those losses and reframed it as spiritual calling: "I don't need solace from anyone, only from God." That's a defense mechanism wearing the clothing of philosophy. Your body doesn't believe it. The longing for a partner runs through your vault from beginning to end.

### 1.2 What You've Been Doing Instead

**You built an impossibly precise filter.**

Your requirements: sexually activating + spiritually conscious + purpose-driven + not promiscuous + potentially able to bear children.

These look like standards. They function as armor. No real human arrives pre-sorted this way — because real humans are contradictory, imperfect, and don't reveal their spiritual consciousness in the first conversation. The filter is engineered, probably unconsciously, so that no one can get close enough to hurt you.

There's also a second trap: **you're waiting to be finished before you let someone in.** The idea is that once you're fully integrated, 80kg, app live, judo brown belt, music released, China trip done — then you'll deserve the level of partner you want. But the integration happens *through* the relationship, not before it. The right person doesn't need you complete. She needs you honest.

### 1.3 The Sample Size Problem

Your daily life, based on the vault, looks like this: wake up, train alone, engineer job remotely, work on the app or write alone in the evenings, sleep.

There are no people in that picture. You cannot meet the right partner from inside your apartment. The math doesn't work. You need exposure to humans — which means being in places where humans exist, repeatedly, over time, without an agenda.

She will not emerge from a checklist reviewed in isolation. She will emerge from a life that includes other humans.

### 1.4 The Sexuality Question — The Shadow You've Processed the Least

Your shadow work files are honest about this: you're attracted to cisgender women and to trans women. You've framed this as a "problem" to solve, a conflict with your desire for biological children, a question of spiritual purity.

It is not a problem. It is part of who you are.

This one requires a therapist who is LGBTQ-affirming — not because anything is wrong with you, but because this is the shadow you can't process alone. Everything else in your vault, you've analyzed with great depth. This one has been buried. That changes in your best version.

### 1.5 What Actually Works — The Concrete Path

**Step 1: Process the relational wounds before you go looking**

Write about Barbara specifically. Not to contact her — to complete the emotional cycle. What did you feel? What did you wish you'd done differently? What did losing her teach you about how you handle closeness? Do the same for Naya. These aren't just memories — they're active blocks. Until you complete them emotionally, they stay in the doorway.

This goes faster with a therapist than with a journal. Not because journaling is wrong, but because this wound is relational — it needs to be processed *with* another human.

**Step 2: Judo first, partner second**

Judo forces physical contact with strangers. It forces trust — you have to let another person take you to the ground and help you back up, repeatedly. It builds camaraderie through shared difficulty without requiring emotional disclosure. It puts you in the same room, three times a week, with people who will eventually know your name.

Bonds formed in training are some of the deepest human bonds. Some of the people at your judo class will have circles — friends, sisters, people they'll introduce you to. The social world you build there is the ecosystem where the partner eventually appears. Not through looking, but through having a life that includes other humans.

**Step 3: Say yes to two invitations per month you'd normally refuse**

Not a social life overhaul. Two invitations. A barbecue, a dinner, a concert, anything. Your nervous system currently treats groups of humans as potential threats — the crypto collapse rewired it that way. The rewiring doesn't undo through thinking. It undoes through repeated exposure to humans who don't attack you, until the nervous system updates its threat model. Six months of two invitations per month. Your baseline will shift.

**Step 4: Feel attraction before running it through the filter**

When you meet someone you're drawn to — in judo, through a friend, anywhere — let yourself feel it before analyzing it. Don't immediately assess whether she's spiritually conscious. Don't run her through the checklist. Just feel it. The analysis can come much, much later. The feeling comes first. The framework has been killing your ability to encounter real humans. Put it down at the door.

**Step 5: Stop waiting to be finished**

You are 30 years old. You're an AI engineer making $7,600/month. You've lost 38kg. You're building an app, writing music, building a game, developing a philosophical system that came from real transformation. You're not the 127kg version of yourself. You're not a disaster waiting to be cleaned up before someone can see you.

The version of you that exists right now — imperfect, mid-journey, with wounds that haven't fully healed — is enough to be in a relationship. She doesn't need the arrived version. She needs the honest one.

Show up.
