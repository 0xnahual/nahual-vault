## PART 3: THE MARTIAL ART QUESTION

### Should you pursue Judo + Streetlifting + Chen Tai Chi as the path to teaching?

**Short answer: Judo yes. Streetlifting yes. Chen Tai Chi not yet. And the goal shouldn't be combining them into "your own martial art" — at least not for years.**

Here's why, brutally:

**Judo — YES, NOW**
- Gives you real combat competence (throws, groundwork, randori under pressure)
- Teaches you to be thrown, to fail, to recover — which your philosophical framework talks about but you haven't lived physically
- Has a belt system that gives you external accountability and milestones
- Makes you a REAL martial artist, not someone who does kung fu stances in his room
- Footage of you doing judo in a real dojo is 100x more credible than solo stance videos
- Judo is the missing "beast" in your system — you have lots of "spirit" training (Tai Chi, Qi Gong, meditation) but no hard combat

**Streetlifting / Calisthenics — YES, NOW**
- Your calisthenics standards file shows you already understand progressive overload
- Weighted pull-ups, dips, muscle-ups = visible strength that builds credibility
- Streetlifting culture is huge on social media, especially in Latin America
- A guy who does weighted pull-ups AND judo AND kung fu stances is visually interesting
- It bridges the gap between "spiritual training" and "actually strong"

**Chen Tai Chi — NOT YET**
- You have a detailed 4-phase curriculum written out, but you're self-teaching from the internet
- Chen Tai Chi done wrong is just arm waving. Done right, it takes years of in-person instruction with a lineage teacher
- Your vault shows you understand the theory (silk reeling, fajin, zhan zhuang) but your transformation journal shows you can't touch your feet and recently couldn't get off the floor without hands
- Chen Tai Chi requires deep structural integrity that you're still building
- If you train it now without a teacher, you'll develop bad habits that are nearly impossible to fix

**The "own martial art" question:**
Creating your own martial art requires:
1. Deep competence in multiple systems (10+ years training each)
2. Combat testing (you need to have FOUGHT, not just trained)
3. Teaching experience (hundreds of students, not just content)
4. A unique technical contribution (not just philosophy — an actual movement innovation)

You're at year ~1 of serious physical training after a long break. You're 105kg. You have zero competition experience. You have zero teaching experience with real students.

**The honest path:**
- Train judo 3x/week for 3-5 years. Get your blue/brown belt.
- Train calisthenics seriously. Get to weighted muscle-ups, front lever, handstand.
- Find a Chen Tai Chi teacher when your body is ready (probably 1-2 years from now).
- Teach IXNAH as what it actually is: a FOUNDATIONAL MOVEMENT SYSTEM for people who want to prepare their bodies for martial arts, not a martial art itself.
- In 5-10 years, when you have real competence in judo + calisthenics + Tai Chi + your IXNAH foundation, THEN you can start integrating them into something new.

Calling it "your own martial art" now, before you've earned rank in any art, would destroy your credibility with anyone who actually trains. Don't do it.

## PART 4: SOCIAL MEDIA PLAN

### Platform Strategy

**Primary: TikTok/Instagram Reels (Spanish)**
This is where your audience lives. Mexican, Spanish-speaking, interested in training, spirituality, self-improvement. Short-form video is where you demonstrate, not explain.

**Secondary: YouTube (Spanish)**
Longer content: training vlogs, philosophical essays narrated, transformation documentation.

**Tertiary: Twitter/X**
Written thoughts, philosophical one-liners, links to longer content. Not primary — you tend to overthink on Twitter.

### Content Pillars (2 videos/week minimum)

**Pillar 1: "Posturas que te cambian la vida" (40% of content)**
- Film yourself doing IXNAH postures with timer on screen
- Show the struggle, the shaking, the progression
- Titles like: "Aguanté 3 minutos en Ma Bu — día 47" / "Dead hang de 2 minutos: lo que tu espalda necesita"
- No explanation. No philosophy. Just body + timer + progression.
- This is your top-of-funnel. Simple, visual, shareable.

**Pillar 2: "El camino" — Transformation documentation (30% of content)**
- Monthly weigh-ins, body composition
- Training footage: judo class, streetlifting park sessions, stance practice
- Honest failures: "Comí 5 gorditas hoy, así se ve la realidad"
- This is your trust-builder. People follow transformations, not teachers.

**Pillar 3: "Hechicería práctica" — Bite-sized philosophy (20% of content)**
- 60-second narrated pieces:
  - "Una gran responsabilidad conlleva un gran poder" (the Spider-Man inversion)
  - "Tu sistema nervioso tiene dos modos: guerrero y místico"
  - "La risa es el hechizo más poderoso"
- Short, quotable, memorable. Film with atmospheric lighting, speak directly to camera.

**Pillar 4: "Judo diaries" — Combat footage (10% of content)**
- Getting thrown. Throwing someone. Learning. Failing.
- This separates you from every other "spiritual fitness" account
- Shows you're actually in the arena, not just philosophizing

### Content Rules
1. Film in Spanish. Always. Your English content is generic; your Spanish is alive.
2. Never explain what IXNAH is in a video. Show it. Let people ask.
3. No spiritual language in video titles. Use body-first hooks: "Esta postura te arregla la cadera en 30 días" not "Despierta tu energía ancestral."
4. Show meals. Show the bad ones too. Authenticity > aspiration.
5. Post schedule: Tuesday + Friday, 6pm Mexico City time.

### Growth Milestones
- **Month 1-3:** 20-30 videos posted. Expect 100-500 followers. Don't measure success yet. Build the library.
- **Month 3-6:** Start seeing which posture videos get traction. Double down on those. Expect 500-2,000 followers.
- **Month 6-12:** If content is consistent and authentic, 2,000-10,000 followers is realistic. Start building email list.
- **Month 12+:** Evaluate: is the audience asking for more? That's when you sell the IXNAH program.
