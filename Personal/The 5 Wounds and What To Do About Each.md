## THE 5 WOUNDS AND WHAT TO DO ABOUT EACH

### WOUND 1: The Crypto Collapse / Public Humiliation

**What it is:** You were a top-5 crypto influencer, made millions by 25, then a project failed. You lost money, employees, your best friend, your reputation. People attacked you online. You weren't "impecable" with your actions — your word, avarice.

**What it's still doing to you:** Every time you think about posting content, showing your face, or putting yourself out there publicly, this wound activates. It's not "I don't want to be famous" — it's "I'm terrified of being destroyed again." Your entire fame/anonymity debate across dozens of files is this wound talking. The "faceless content" strategy is partially genuine preference and partially avoidance disguised as philosophy.

**What to do:**

1. **Name the specific people and moments.** In a private document (not for publication), write out exactly what happened. Who did you hurt? Who hurt you? What did you do that was "not impecable"? What were the specific investments that failed? What did people say about you online? Right now this wound lives as a vague cloud of shame. Shame survives in vagueness. It dies in specificity.
    
2. **Write letters you'll never send.** To your former best friend. To the employees who lost their jobs. To the people who talked shit. Not to justify yourself — to acknowledge what happened from their perspective. This isn't about forgiveness yet. It's about fully seeing the event from every angle so it stops being a monster under the bed.
    
3. **Therapy — specifically EMDR or somatic experiencing.** Your vault shows you can do extraordinary introspective work alone. But this wound has a trauma signature (phobic avoidance of public visibility, physical anxiety responses). Trauma doesn't resolve through thinking about it — it resolves through processing it in the nervous system. EMDR is fast (6-12 sessions for a specific event) and evidence-based. A bilingual therapist in Mexico who does EMDR would be ideal. This is the single highest-ROI action for your psychological health.
    
4. **Separate the lesson from the punishment.** You learned something real: be impeccable with your word, don't be avaricious, don't abandon your responsibilities as a leader. Good. But you've been punishing yourself for 2+ years with isolation, weight gain, and self-denial. The lesson is complete. The punishment needs to end. You can write a declaration: "I was wrong. I learned. The sentence is over."

### WOUND 2: Relational Abandonment Pattern

**What it is:** Your subconscious told you directly: "You leave people too easily. That's why you're alone." Naya, Barbara, others — you let them go. Now you can't open up to new people because the last time you did, it ended in loss.

**What it's still doing to you:** You stay home. You don't accept invitations. You set impossibly high standards for a partner (must be sexually activating + spiritually conscious + purpose-driven). You frame being alone as spiritual discipline ("I don't need solace from anyone, only from God") when part of it is just fear.

**What to do:**

1. **Go where humans are, weekly, with no agenda.** Judo is perfect for this. Judo forces physical contact, trust, vulnerability (you will be thrown, repeatedly), and camaraderie — without requiring emotional disclosure. It's the Bestia's medicine: primal, physical connection with other humans. Don't skip this. 2-3 times per week minimum.
    
2. **Say yes to 2 invitations per month that you'd normally refuse.** Not every invitation. Just two. The bar is low: a barbecue, a gym session with someone, coffee. You don't need to "open up." Just be present in a room with other people. Your subconscious was right — you've been hiding.
    
3. **Stop engineering the ideal partner and start having imperfect human contact.** Your requirements list (sexually activating + conscious + purposeful + not promiscuous + potentially able to bear children) is a defense mechanism. Nobody passes this filter because the filter is designed to keep everyone out. You don't need to lower your standards. You need to meet 50 new people so your nervous system remembers that human connection isn't dangerous. The partner will emerge from that — not from a checklist.
    
4. **Grieve Barbara specifically.** Your subconscious named her. She matched your "soul level." You let her go. There's unfinished emotional business there. Write about what happened. What you felt. What you wish you'd done differently. Not to contact her — to complete the emotional cycle so it stops blocking new connections.

### WOUND 3: The Shame-Binge Cycle (Addiction Infrastructure)

**What it is:** The addictions themselves may be largely resolved, but the _emotional regulation pattern_ underneath them is still active. The cycle: feel pain/emptiness/anxiety → seek immediate relief (substance, masturbation, trading) → binge → shame → feel worse → repeat. You've replaced the substances with discipline, which is good, but the moment discipline cracks, the old pattern fires.

**What it's still doing to you:** Day 3 of your transformation journal: "me masturbe 5 veces... no entrene." Day 5: "deja el trading ya." The pattern didn't disappear — it's dormant. And the shame you feel after a "relapse" is disproportionate because it triggers the deeper shame of the crypto collapse. One slip becomes evidence that you're still "that person."

**What to do:**

1. **Build a relapse protocol, not a relapse prevention fantasy.** You will slip. Everyone does. The damage isn't the slip — it's the shame spiral that follows. Write a protocol for yourself: "When I binge (masturbation, food, whatever), I will: (a) not do it a second time that day, (b) not judge myself, (c) train the next morning regardless, (d) write one paragraph about what I was feeling BEFORE the binge." The "before" is where the real information is.
    
2. **Learn to sit with discomfort without solving it.** Your entire vault shows a pattern: feel something difficult → immediately try to transmute it (write about it, philosophize it, invoke the Espiritu, invoke the Bestia, make a framework). Sometimes the right response to pain is just... feeling it. For 10 minutes. Without writing. Without invoking anything. Without turning it into content. The ability to tolerate uncomfortable emotions without acting on them is the specific skill that prevents relapse. Meditation helps here, but specifically **noting meditation** (Shinzen Young style) — where you label what you feel ("sadness," "anxiety," "boredom," "lust") without doing anything about it.
    
3. **Decouple discipline from identity.** Right now, when you train and eat well, you feel like "your best version." When you don't, you feel like a failure. This means your entire self-worth rides on daily performance. That's unsustainable. You need a baseline identity that isn't contingent on today's behavior. Something like: "I am Ricardo. I am a creator and a fighter. Some days I'm great at it, some days I'm not. Both are fine. The trajectory matters, not the day."

### WOUND 4: The Grandiosity-Deflation Oscillation

**What it is:** When the Espiritu takes over, you write things like "I will be one of the greatest artists in history," "I have infinite power," "I am a God." When reality doesn't match, the crash is brutal — and the crash feeds the addictions. This isn't just confidence vs. self-doubt. It's an inflated spiritual identity that creates expectations no human can meet, followed by a collapse that feels like God abandoned you.

**What it's still doing to you:** It makes every creative project carry existential weight. You're not just making an app — you're fulfilling God's mission. You're not just writing songs — you're transmitting divine messages. This pressure makes it nearly impossible to ship anything, because "good enough" doesn't exist when God is watching. It also makes every failure feel like a spiritual betrayal rather than a normal setback.

**What to do:**

1. **Practice smallness.** Deliberately do things that have zero spiritual significance. Cook a meal. Walk without purpose. Watch something stupid. Clean a room. Not everything needs to be part of the mission. Your psyche needs rest from meaning. The Espiritu won't give you that rest — you have to take it.
    
2. **When you catch yourself writing in messianic language, stop and rewrite it as a normal human.** "I will be one of the greatest artists in history" → "I want to make art that matters to people." "I have infinite power" → "I feel strong today and I want to use it well." "God told me to lose 20kg" → "I know I'll think more clearly if I'm lighter." Same intention, grounded language. The grandiosity isn't protecting you — it's setting you up for crashes.
    
3. **Ship something imperfect.** The antidote to grandiosity is humility, and the fastest path to humility is releasing something that isn't perfect. Ship [[Kung Fu Temple App Spec|El Templo]] at 80%. Let people use it. Let them find bugs. Let it be mediocre in some areas. The world will not end. God will not be disappointed. And you'll learn that you survive imperfection, which is the lesson your psyche actually needs.

### WOUND 5: Isolation as Spiritual Identity

**What it is:** You've built an identity around solitude — the lone warrior, the shaman who walks between worlds, the artist who doesn't need anyone. Your writings explicitly valorize not needing "solace" from humans, only from God. But your subconscious keeps screaming the opposite: "Sal de tu casa. Habla con gente. Deja de ser mamón."

**What it's still doing to you:** You have no close friends documented in the vault. The people mentioned (Jordy, Dani, Vibing crew) appear in older entries. Your daily life appears to be: train alone, code alone, write alone, eat alone. The Espiritu tells you this is noble. Your body is telling you (through weight, migraines, energy fluctuations) that it's not.

**What to do:**

1. **Find one person you can be honest with.** Not a therapist (though that too). A friend. Someone who knows about the crypto past, the addictions, the spiritual journey, and doesn't need you to be impressive. If this person doesn't exist yet, judo is where you'll find them. Training together creates bonds faster than talking.
    
2. **Stop using God as a substitute for human connection.** This is the hardest one. Your relationship with God is real and valuable. But when "God is my only solace" becomes "I don't need people," it's a defense mechanism wearing spiritual clothing. God made you a social animal. The divine within you WANTS human connection — your writings prove it (the longing for a partner, the desire for an audience, the dream of a foundation). Honoring God means honoring your full humanity, including the part that needs other humans.
    
3. **Vulnerability practice: tell one person one real thing per week.** Not on paper. Not to your subconscious. To a human being's face. "I've been struggling with X." "I feel lonely sometimes." "I'm proud of Y." The content doesn't matter. The act of being seen does. This is the muscle you've let atrophy the most.
