# Chen Style Tai Chi — Learning Order (Lock-In Plan)

**Follow this exactly. No improvising. No style hopping.**

---

## Phase 1 — Build the Engine (8–12 weeks)
**Do NOT learn forms yet. Build the body first.**

### Daily (20–30 min)
- **Zhan Zhuang (Standing) – 5–10 min**
  - Feet rooted
  - Knees soft
  - Spine long
  - Breath quiet

- **Silk Reeling Basics (Chan Si Gong) – 10–15 min**
  - Waist circles
  - Arm spirals
  - Opening / closing
  - Slow, continuous

- **Weight Shifting – 5–10 min**
  - Empty / full
  - Slow stepping
  - Hips lead

**Rule:**  
If you skip this phase, everything later is fake.

---

## Phase 2 — Short Form (8–16 weeks)
**Only start when Phase 1 feels natural.**

### Daily (20–40 min)
- **Chen Short Form (Laojia Yilu – short version)**
  - Slow
  - Clean
  - No speed yet
  - Memorize without rushing

- **Silk Reeling – 5–10 min**
  - Always keep this

**Rule:**  
Form = moving silk reeling.

---

## Phase 3 — Power (after 4–6 months)
**You earn speed only after structure is real.**

### 2–3x per week
- Fajin drills
- Punches from silk reeling
- Stomps
- Short explosive releases

**Rule:**  
Few reps. Stop before tension appears.

---

## Phase 4 — Integration (forever)
Now everything connects:
- Staff = Chen
- Kicks spiral
- Judo grips root
- Walking becomes Tai Chi
- Singing breath deepens
- Camera presence locks in

---

## Weekly Structure (Simple)
- **Daily**: Standing + silk reeling
- **Daily**: Short form (when unlocked)
- **2–3x/week**: Fajin
- **Shaolin class**: keep it
- **Judo**: keep it
- **Staff**: 3–4x/week

---

## What NOT to Do
- Don’t learn multiple forms
- Don’t chase YouTube masters
- Don’t add speed early
- Don’t collect drills
- Don’t skip standing
- Don’t train without silence

---

## Final Rule
If you ask “what should I learn next?”  
The answer is: **go deeper into the current phase.**

Train.
