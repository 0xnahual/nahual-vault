# Compression Rebuild Protocol  
**(Fixes L-Sit · Pike · Pancake · Press Bottleneck)**

**Core truth:**  
This is not flexibility. This is **active compression + pelvic control**.  
Train it **daily**, before strength or martial arts.

---

## DAILY ROUTINE (15–20 min)
**Do BEFORE everything else.**

### 1️⃣ Seated Pike Compression Lifts
- Sit on floor, legs straight  
- Hands by hips  
- Posterior pelvic tilt (slight lower-back round)  
- Lift heels even **1–2 cm**

**2–3 sets × 5–8 reps**  
Hold **3–5s** per rep  
> Small lift still counts.

---

### 2️⃣ Pike Hold (Primary Skill – Exposure, not max)
- Hands on floor or parallettes  
- Legs straight  
- Hips high  
- Calm breathing  
- Stop at **panic**, not pain

**3–4 sets × 20–40s**

If cramp appears:
- stop  
- shake out  
- resume lighter

Goal = confidence.

---

### 3️⃣ Tuck L-Sit Holds
- Parallettes or blocks  
- Knees tucked to chest  
- Shoulders depressed  
- Posterior pelvic tilt

**4–6 holds × 10–20s**

Cue: *pull thighs into chest*.

---

### 4️⃣ Active Pancake Pulses
- Wide straddle  
- Hands on floor in front  
- Chest up  
- Actively pull forward  
- Optional: light heel lift

**2–3 sets × 10–15 slow pulses**

No bouncing. No collapse.

---

## OPTIONAL ADD-ONS (2–3× / week)

### Hip Flexor Isometric
- Sit tall  
- One leg straight, other bent  
- Lift straight leg

**3 holds × 20–30s per leg**

---

### Eccentric L-Sit Lowers
- Start tuck  
- Slowly extend legs  
- Return to tuck

**3 sets × 5 slow reps**

---

## RULES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

1. **Compression comes first. Always.**  
2. **Calm breathing = progress.**  
3. **Cramps = nervous system learning.** Stop, reset, continue.  
4. **Finish feeling more capable**, not wrecked.  
5. **Daily exposure beats intensity.**

---

## PHASE TIMELINE
- **Weeks 1–3:** Own basics, reduce cramps, gain control  
- **Weeks 4–6:** Longer holds, smoother transitions, press work unlocks

---

## ANCHOR
**Compression is not a stretch.  
It’s a skill.  
Repetition + calm = permission.**
