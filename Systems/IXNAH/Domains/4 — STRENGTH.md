# IXNAH Domain 4: STRENGTH

*Raw force production. The ability to push, pull, lift, carry, grip, and resist load — loaded and unloaded, with your body and with objects.*

---

## Definition

Strength in IXNAH is not bodybuilding. It is not hypertrophy for aesthetics. It is not powerlifting for numbers. It is the functional capacity to exert force against resistance — your own body, another person, a heavy object, gravity.

A capable human must be able to: push their bodyweight off the ground, pull themselves over a wall, carry another person, grip something and not let go, stand up under load, and resist being moved. These are not gym goals. These are survival and capability requirements that happen to be trainable in a gym (or a park, or with a bar and some weights).

IXNAH's strength philosophy: **Mastery → Load → Mastery.** Own the movement pattern before you add weight. The Progression Law: slow it down → add pauses → add range → add weight → repeat.

---

## What Strength Contains

### Upper Body (Compendium Part 2, Chapter 4)

**Push (horizontal):**
- Wall pushup → Incline → Standard → Deficit → Diamond → Explosive → Weighted
- 100 pushups protocol (daily practice, varies grip/speed/tempo)
- Standards at each level

**Push (vertical/inverted):**
- Pike pushup → Wall handstand → Handstand hold → Handstand pushup
- Why: shoulder health, full-body tension, proprioception

**Pull:**
- Body row → Pullup → Chin-up → Weighted pullup → Muscle-up
- Gi/towel pull-ups for judo-specific grip strength
- Russian method: progressive reps

**Dip & Press:**
- Bench dip → Parallel bars → Weighted → Ring dips
- Connection to clinch control and frame strength

**Integration:**
- Shaolin crawl (side-to-side lizard crawl: push-pull-core combined)

### Lower Body (Compendium Part 2, Chapter 5)

**Squat progressions:**
- Bodyweight → Bulgarian split → Cossack → Pistol
- Connection to Ma Bu, Gong Bu, Pu Bu (stances ARE squat progressions)

**Heavy hip hinge (when added to IXNAH):**
- Deadlift / kettlebell swing — the most fundamental human movement pattern
- Transfer to judo throwing, self-defense, longevity
- Minimum: 1-2x/week

**Loaded carries (when added):**
- Farmer's walks, sandbag carries, rucking
- Grip endurance + core stability + real-world functional strength

### Grip (Compendium Part 2, Chapter 6)

**Why grip is critical:**
- #1 predictor of all-cause mortality in elderly (hard science, not bro-science)
- #1 factor in judo (no grip = no throw)
- Essential for climbing, carrying, controlling

**The grip protocol:**
- Dead hang progressions (passive → active → one-arm)
- Gi/towel pull-ups
- Plate pinch holds
- Fat grip hangs
- Farmer's walks
- Rice bucket (forearm prehab)
- Frequency: 10-15 minutes, 3x/week

### Skill Work

- L-sit hold
- Front lever hold
- Planche lean
- Handstand hold

## Connection to IXNAH Pillars

- **Voluntad (Will)** — Strength training IS will training. The last rep of a set, the final seconds of a hang, the decision to add weight when everything says stop. Will made physical.
- **Energia (Energy)** — Strength is energy applied against resistance. Managing energy output (when to push, when to rest, when to explode) is a direct application.
- **Balance** — Not just physical balance under load, but the balance between training hard enough to grow and resting enough to recover.

## How It Maps to El Templo

- **Discipline:** Fuerza (with sub-categories: Push, Pull, Dips, Skill, Lower Body)
- **Exercise types:** Reps (pushups, pullups, squats), Timer (holds, hangs), Weighted (weighted pull, weighted dip)
- **Mastery paths:** Calisthenics progression trees — each progression is a skill tree branch
- **Medicine text examples:** Pushup → "horizontal pressing power, shoulder stability." Pullup → "vertical pulling strength, lat engagement, grip endurance."

## The Honest Assessment

Strength is decent in IXNAH — calisthenics progressions are well-documented and proven on your body. What's missing: heavy lifting (deadlift, carries, Turkish Get-Up) and dedicated grip training. These fill in as you train them. The calisthenics base is real and solid.
