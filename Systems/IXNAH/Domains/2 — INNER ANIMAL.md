# IXNAH Domain 2: INNER ANIMAL

*Back to monke. The primal physicality that civilization stole. Not aggression toward humans — raw animal capability that makes the body dangerous without needing to fight.*

---

## Definition

INNER ANIMAL is the domain of primal physical capability. Climbing, brachiating, crawling, sprinting, gripping, dragging, swimming, throwing objects — the things a gorilla can do that a gym-trained human cannot. The jungle, not the gym.

This is NOT aggression. An [[What is IXNAH|IXNAH]] warrior is not aggressive — he is a fucking animal who CAN neutralize you, but he will not attack you. INNER ANIMAL builds the raw, unstructured, primal capacity that makes the body dangerous. [[3 — DEFENDING|DEFENDING]] (Domain 3) decides when and how to use it. The animal qualities make him capable. The defending philosophy makes him restrained.

The body was not designed to sit in chairs and lift calibrated weights. It was designed to climb trees, sprint from threats, crawl through terrain, grip branches, carry loads over uneven ground, and swim through water. INNER ANIMAL reclaims what the modern world erased.

---

## What INNER ANIMAL Contains

### Climbing & Brachiating
- Tree climbing, wall climbing, rope climbing
- Brachiating (swinging hand to hand — monkey bars, branches)
- Vertical pulling in natural environments (not just a pullup bar)

### Crawling & Ground Movement
- Bear crawl, army crawl, crab walk — on actual terrain
- Moving through, under, and over obstacles
- Low-to-ground locomotion at speed

### Sprinting & Explosive Locomotion
- Short sprints (30-60m) — predator speed, distance closing
- Direction changes, cuts, reactive sprints
- The burst that closes the gap between standing apart and clinch in half a second

### Gorilla Grip
- Hanging from anything — bars, branches, ledges
- Grip endurance under unpredictable conditions
- Gripping objects that aren't designed to be gripped (rocks, poles, fabric)
- The clinch grip that doesn't release

### Dragging & Carrying (Unstructured)
- Dragging heavy objects (tires, sandbags, logs)
- Carrying awkward loads (stones, sandbags bear-hugged)
- Moving heavy things through space without equipment
- Different from Farmer's Carry (Strength) — this is raw, messy, environmental

### Swimming
- Open water movement
- Breath control under physical exertion
- Full-body coordination in a non-standard medium

### Throwing Objects
- Rocks, medicine balls, sandbags — throwing heavy things for distance or accuracy
- Explosive full-body power through an external object
- Rotational power in an uncontrolled context

## How INNER ANIMAL Differs from Other Domains

| INNER ANIMAL | STRENGTH | MOVING |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| Unstructured, environmental | Structured, progressive | Controlled, range-focused |
| Outdoors, unpredictable | Gym or park, repeatable | Mat or floor, precise |
| Primal capacity | Measurable force output | Quality of movement |
| No equipment required | KB, pullup bar, sandbag | Body only |

[[4 — STRENGTH|STRENGTH]] is the gym gorilla. INNER ANIMAL is the jungle gorilla. The gym gorilla can deadlift 200 kg but can't climb a tree. The jungle gorilla can do both — because the environment demanded it.

---

## Mastery Standards

There are no reps or sets. INNER ANIMAL mastery is demonstrated, not tested:

| Capability | Standard |
|-----------|----------|
| Climb | Scale a 3-meter wall or tree without assistance |
| Sprint | 40m sprint at maximum speed, explosive start |
| Crawl | 30m bear crawl at speed, low to ground |
| Hang | 2-minute dead hang from any grippable surface |
| Carry | Bear-hug a 40 kg sandbag over 50m of uneven terrain |
| Swim | 200m continuous in open water |
| Throw | Hurl a 10 kg medicine ball 5+ meters |

## Connection to IXNAH Pillars

- **Energía (Energy)** — INNER ANIMAL is raw energy output. Unrefined, explosive, primal. The fuel before the form.
- **Voluntad (Will)** — Sprinting when the lungs scream. Hanging when the grip burns. Crawling when the body wants to stop. The animal doesn't negotiate with discomfort.
- **Fluir (Flow)** — Movement without thought. Climbing doesn't allow thinking — the body reacts or the body falls. INNER ANIMAL forces flow because the environment demands it.

---

## How It's Trained

INNER ANIMAL is NOT a gym session. It's trained through:

1. **Environmental training** — go outside. Climb things. Sprint in parks. Crawl on grass. Hang from whatever is available.
2. **Tuesday/Thursday evenings** — the La Revelación slots can include INNER ANIMAL work as an alternative to creative blocks
3. **Weekend play** — unstructured outdoor movement. No plan. Just a body in an environment, moving like an animal.
4. **Judo overlap** — gripping the gi IS gorilla grip. Being thrown IS impact conditioning. Randori scrambles ARE primal ground movement under pressure.

## The Honest Assessment

INNER ANIMAL is currently the least formalized domain. That's partially by design — primal movement resists systematization. But it also needs more actual training time. The risk is that it becomes the domain that sounds cool in documents but gets skipped for structured gym work. The fix: schedule it. Tuesday or Thursday evenings. Go outside. Climb something. Sprint. Crawl. The animal doesn't live in Obsidian — it lives in the mud.
