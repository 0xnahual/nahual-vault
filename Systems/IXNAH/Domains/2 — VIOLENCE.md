# IXNAH Domain 2: VIOLENCE

*The ability to hurt someone when necessary. Not aggression. Not anger. Controlled, trained, purposeful capacity for physical force against another human.*

---

## Definition

Violence is the domain most people either glorify or avoid. IXNAH does neither. A capable human must be able to generate force against another person — not because they want to, but because the inability to do so makes them vulnerable. A person who cannot fight is not choosing peace. They are choosing helplessness and calling it peace.

IXNAH's approach to violence is clinical: learn to strike, learn to throw, learn to control. Train it under pressure with real humans. Then — ideally — never use it. The paradox of martial arts: the more capable you are of violence, the less likely you are to need it. Trained fighters de-escalate. Untrained people panic.

---

## What Violence Contains

### Striking (from Boxing — Compendium Part 3, Chapter 8)

**The 4 punches:**
- Jab — range finder, setup, defense, scoring
- Cross — power shot, rear hand, hip rotation
- Hook — close range, around the guard, liver shot
- Uppercut — rising strike, inside line, when opponent ducks

**Combinations:**
- Jab-Cross (the 1-2)
- Jab-Cross-Hook (the 1-2-3)
- Jab-Body-Head (level change)
- Jab-Cross-Low Kick (the Dutch combination — bridges to kicks)

**Defense with the hands:**
- Guard (high hands, elbows tight)
- Slip (head off centerline)
- Roll (bob and weave under hooks)
- Parry (redirect straight punches)
- Pull back (lean away)

### Kicking (Compendium Part 3, Chapter 9)

**The 4 practical kicks:**
- Teep (front push kick) — distance management, stopping advances
- Low roundhouse — leg destruction, pain compliance, safest kick
- Body roundhouse — fight-ending power, liver
- Side kick — longest range, structural push

**Checking kicks:**
- Shin block against incoming kicks
- Step check

### Clinch (Compendium Part 3, Chapter 10)

**Entries:**
- From a jab-cross: miss → step in → underhook
- From a kick: catch → step in → body lock
- From defense: slip → collar tie → inside position

**Clinch weapons (Muay Thai):**
- Collar tie → knee
- Body clinch → knee to body
- Dirty boxing (short hooks/uppercuts inside)

### Throwing (from Judo — Compendium Part 3, Chapter 11)

**Kuzushi (off-balancing):** 8 directions
**Ashi-waza (foot techniques):** Osoto gari, Ouchi gari, Kouchi gari, Deashi barai
**Koshi-waza (hip throws):** O-goshi, Harai goshi
**Te-waza (hand throws):** Tai otoshi, Seoi nage
**Combination throwing:** Chaining failed attacks into successful ones

## How Violence Is Trained

Violence is NOT trained through theory, files, or frameworks. It is trained through:

1. **Judo (2x/week)** — live throwing against resisting humans
2. **Heavy bag (2x/week)** — striking power and combinations
3. **Sparring (when ready)** — applying technique under pressure
4. **Randori** — judo's live practice, ego destruction, real-time problem solving

The combat layer of IXNAH fills from the mat, not from the keyboard. Document as you train.

---

## Mastery Standards

| Test | Standard |
|------|----------|
| Punch | 3 × 3-min bag rounds, clean technique, combinations |
| Kick | Low kick + teep, both sides, with power |
| Throw | 3 different judo throws in randori (live sparring) |
| Clinch | Enter clinch from striking range, control position |
| Combinations | Flow between punches and kicks without freezing |

## Connection to IXNAH Pillars

- **Voluntad (Will)** — Violence requires will to engage when the body screams to retreat. Holding ground when being hit, entering when being pushed.
- **Intencion (Intention)** — A strike without intention is a flail. Every punch, kick, and throw must have direction and purpose.
- **Energia (Energy)** — Violence is explosive energy expenditure. Managing your energy in a fight (when to explode, when to conserve) is the practical application of this pillar.

## The Honest Assessment

Violence is IXNAH's weakest domain. The Compendium has a system flow ("Strike → Entry → Grip → Kuzushi → Throw → End") but the technique files are mostly empty. This is correct — you're currently training judo and will fill this domain from lived experience. The gap is acknowledged, not hidden. That honesty IS the credibility.
