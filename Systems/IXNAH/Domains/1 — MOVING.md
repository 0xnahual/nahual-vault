# IXNAH Domain 1: MOVING

*Locomotion, agility, ground transitions, climbing, crawling, jumping. The ability to get from here to there — on any surface, in any direction, under any condition.*

---

## Definition

Moving is the most fundamental human capability. Before you can fight, lift, endure, or defend — you must be able to move your body through space with control, efficiency, and adaptability. A human who cannot move well cannot do anything else well.

IXNAH defines Moving not as exercise but as reclaimed birthright. Every child can crawl, climb, squat, jump, roll, and balance. Most adults have lost these abilities through chairs, shoes, and inactivity. Moving is the process of getting them back — and then expanding them.

---

## What Moving Contains

### Foundation Layer (from Part 1 of the Compendium)

**The 7 Medicine Postures** — the starting point for all movement:
- Ma Bu (Horse Stance) — bilateral load, rooting
- Gong Bu (Forward Stance) — unilateral load, gait
- Pu Bu (Drop Stance) — deep range, yielding
- Asian Squat — natural resting position, ankle-hip integration
- Dead Hang — spinal decompression, shoulder reset
- Tree Pose — single-leg balance, vertical integrity
- Forward Fold — hip hinge, posterior chain lengthening

**The 4 Sacred Mobility Portals** — the gates to full-body movement freedom:
- Hip Portal (Gate of Power) — squatting, kicking, throwing, ground movement
- Hamstring Portal (Bowstring of the Warrior) — folding, kicking height, sprint mechanics
- Thoracic Portal (Axis of Spirit) — rotational power, breathing depth, posture
- Ankle Portal (Root of the Beast) — squat depth, running, balance, rooting

**Barefoot Mastery** — rebuilding the foundation:
- Transition protocol (weeks 1-8)
- Forefoot strike mechanics
- Spring-loaded movement
- Silent movement

### Advanced Moving

- Ground transitions (sitting to standing, rolling, crawling)
- Terrain variety (grass, trails, sand, uneven ground, stairs, inclines)
- Basic obstacle navigation (vault, climb, balance, land)
- Low-light / eyes-closed proprioceptive work
- Swimming as movement capability (survival swimming, not competitive)

## Mastery Standards

| Test | Standard |
|------|----------|
| Ma Bu | 2 min at parallel, calm breathing |
| Asian Squat | 5 min comfortable, heels flat |
| Dead Hang | 60 sec passive, controlled breathing |
| Tree Pose | 60 sec each side, eyes closed |
| Forward Fold | Palms flat on floor, relaxed |
| Ground-to-standing | 3 different methods, smooth, no hands if possible |
| Barefoot run | 2 km on mixed terrain, no pain |
| Swim | 200m continuous, any stroke |

## Connection to IXNAH Pillars

- **Presencia** — Moving well requires inhabiting the body fully. You cannot move with precision if your attention is elsewhere.
- **Fluir** — Moving without friction. When the body moves well, it flows. Stiffness is anti-flow.
- **Atencion** — Where you look determines where you move. Awareness of space, terrain, obstacles.

## The Honest Assessment (from the Gap Analysis)

Moving is IXNAH's strongest domain. The 7 postures, barefoot protocol, mobility portals, and calisthenics skill work are all well-documented. What's weak: terrain variety, environmental training, swimming. These develop through lived experience, not more writing.
