## PART 1: THE FOUNDATION — RESTORING THE HUMAN BODY

*Before you can fight, lift, run, or endure — you must be able to stand, squat, hang, fold, and balance. This is where every capable human begins.*

### Chapter 1: The 7 Medicine Postures
*IXNAH V0.1 — the system that took me from 127kg to 89kg. These postures don't build warriors. They build humans who can move again.*

- 1.1 — Why postures, not exercises ⚠️
  - The difference between training movement patterns vs training muscles
  - Postures as diagnostic tools: where you fail tells you what's broken
  - How long-hold isometrics rebuild tendons, joints, and the nervous system

- 1.2 — Postura del Guardián / Ma Bu (Horse Stance) ✅
  - What it is: bilateral load acceptance, knee-hip tolerance
  - How to do it: foot placement, depth, spine position, breathing
  - Common failures and corrections
  - Progressions: wall-assisted → parallel thighs → weighted → timed holds
  - Mastery test: 2-minute hold at parallel, calm breathing
  - What it gives you: confidence under load, standing tolerance, calm under compression

- 1.3 — Postura del Avance / Gong Bu (Forward Stance) ✅
  - What it is: unilateral load, basic gait mechanics
  - How to do it: front knee over ankle, rear leg straight, hips square
  - Common failures and corrections
  - Progressions: shallow → deep → weighted → dynamic transitions
  - Mastery test: unilateral stability, clean transitions between sides
  - What it gives you: clean gait, hip-knee coordination, forward projection

- 1.4 — Postura del Descenso / Pu Bu (Drop Stance) ⚠️
  - What it is: controlled deep range, yielding capacity
  - How to do it: one leg bent deep, other leg extended, both feet flat
  - Common failures and corrections
  - Progressions: elevated heel → full flat → loaded → dynamic
  - Mastery test: controlled descent and rise, both sides
  - What it gives you: safety in deep ranges, hip mobility under control

- 1.5 — Postura del Origen / Asian Squat (Deep Squat) ✅
  - What it is: ankle-hip integration, the natural human resting position
  - How to do it: heels down (or elevated), knees tracking toes, spine neutral
  - Common failures: heels lifting, knees caving, rounding lower back
  - Progressions: heels elevated → flat → loaded → prolonged sitting
  - Mastery test: 5 minutes comfortable with flat heels
  - What it gives you: ankle dorsiflexion, hip tolerance, cartilage lubrication

- 1.6 — Postura del Vacío / Dead Hang ✅
  - What it is: spinal decompression, shoulder reset, grip foundation
  - How to do it: bar grip, shoulders relaxed (passive) or engaged (active)
  - Common failures: death grip, holding breath, shrugging
  - Progressions: partial (feet on ground) → full passive → active → one-arm
  - Mastery test: 60-second passive hang, controlled breathing
  - What it gives you: shoulder comfort, spinal lengthening, grip tolerance

- 1.7 — Postura del Eje / Tree Pose (Single-Leg Balance) ✅
  - What it is: single-leg balance, vertical integrity
  - How to do it: one foot on inner thigh or calf, hips level, arms variable
  - Common failures: hip drop, foot on knee, looking down
  - Progressions: foot on calf → thigh → eyes closed → unstable surface
  - Mastery test: 60 seconds each side, eyes closed, calm
  - What it gives you: fall risk reduction, knee alignment, proprioception

- 1.8 — Postura del Pliegue / Forward Fold ✅
  - What it is: forward folding, hip hinge mechanics
  - How to do it: soft knees, fold from hips, let head hang
  - Common failures: rounding from upper back, locking knees
  - Progressions: bent knees → straight → hands to floor → prolonged
  - Mastery test: palms flat on floor, relaxed breathing
  - What it gives you: posterior chain lengthening, safe spine folding

- 1.9 — The medicine posture protocol ⚠️
  - How to train all 7 as a single session (sequence, timing, rest)
  - Daily minimum: 3 postures, 1-2 minutes each
  - Full session: all 7, progressive holds
  - When to move to the next chapter: you pass ALL mastery tests
  - Principles: depth never mandatory, pain is absolute halt, exit calmly > hold longer

### Chapter 8: Hands — The Boxing Layer
*4 punches. Infinite combinations. Your hands are your first weapon.*

- 8.1 — The jab 🥋
  - Mechanics: lead hand, straight line, snap back
  - Uses: range finder, setup, defense, scoring
  - The jab as the most important strike in any fight

- 8.2 — The cross 🥋
  - Mechanics: rear hand, hip rotation, full extension
  - Uses: power shot, follow-up to jab
  - Common errors: dropping the other hand, telegraphing

- 8.3 — The hook 🥋
  - Mechanics: lead hand, horizontal arc, elbow locked at 90°
  - Uses: close range power, around the guard
  - Lead hook to the body — the liver shot

- 8.4 — The uppercut 🥋
  - Mechanics: rising strike, inside line
  - Uses: when opponent ducks or shells up
  - Short range, massive power

- 8.5 — Combinations 🥋
  - Jab-cross (the 1-2: bread and butter)
  - Jab-cross-hook (the 1-2-3: most common knockout combo)
  - Jab-body-head (level change attack)
  - Jab-cross-low kick (the Dutch combination — bridges to kicks)
  - Building your own combinations: principles of flow

- 8.6 — Defense with the hands 🥋
  - Guard (high hands, elbows tight)
  - Slip (head off centerline — inside slip, outside slip)
  - Roll (duck under hooks — the bob and weave)
  - Parry (redirect straight punches with open hand)
  - Pull back (lean away from range — for long fighters)
  - Shoulder roll (advanced — Philly shell defense)

- 8.7 — The heavy bag protocol ⚠️
  - 3 × 3-minute rounds, 1-minute rest
  - Round 1: jab-cross only, focus on technique
  - Round 2: all combinations, add hooks and uppercuts
  - Round 3: free flow, power shots, combinations ending with movement
  - How to train alone without developing bad habits

### Chapter 9: Kicks — The IXNAH Leg Arsenal
*4 kicks. Practical. Trainable at 50. No acrobatics.*

- 9.1 — The teep / front push kick ⚠️
  - Mechanics: chamber, extend, push (not snap)
  - Uses: distance management, stopping advances, setting up hands
  - The teep as IXNAH's signature kick: defensive, powerful, low-risk
  - Jab of the legs

- 9.2 — The low roundhouse kick ⚠️
  - Mechanics: hip rotation, shin contact, follow-through
  - Uses: leg destruction, pain compliance, slowing the opponent
  - The most practical self-defense kick: safe, effective, no balance risk
  - How to throw it after a jab-cross (the Dutch combination)

- 9.3 — The body roundhouse kick ⚠️
  - Mechanics: same as low kick but targeting ribs/liver
  - Uses: fight-ending power, body damage accumulation
  - When to throw it vs when it's too risky

- 9.4 — The side kick ⚠️
  - Mechanics: chamber, extend through the heel, retract
  - Uses: longest range strike, pushing opponent away, structural power
  - Connection to IXNAH stance work: the side kick IS a loaded Gong Bu turned sideways

- 9.5 — Checking kicks 🥋
  - How to defend against incoming kicks: shin block, step check
  - Why this matters: if you can kick, others can kick you
  - Connection to judo: checking a kick and entering the clinch

### Chapter 10: The Clinch — Where Striking Meets Throwing
*The bridge between boxing and judo. This is where IXNAH becomes unique.*

- 10.1 — Entering the clinch 🥋
  - From a jab-cross: miss the cross → step in → underhook

  - The transition: when to stop striking and start throwing

## PART 4: CONDITIONING — THE ENGINE THAT SUSTAINS EVERYTHING

*Strength is useless without the cardio to sustain it. A fight lasts minutes. A life lasts decades. Both require conditioning.*

### Chapter 14: The Three Energy Systems
*Your body runs on three fuels. Train all three.*

- 14.1 — Alactic power (0-10 seconds) ❌
  - What it is: explosive bursts — sprinting, throwing, striking
  - How to train: 6-10 × 30m sprints, full recovery between sets
  - Why it matters: a judo throw happens in 2 seconds. A knockout punch in 0.5 seconds.

- 14.2 — Lactic capacity (30-90 seconds) ❌
  - What it is: sustained high-intensity — a fight round, a scramble, running from danger
  - How to train: heavy bag rounds (3×3min), judo uchikomi rounds, hill sprints
  - Why it matters: randori is 5 minutes of hell. If you gas out, technique disappears.

- 14.3 — Aerobic base (30-60+ minutes) ❌
  - What it is: sustained low-intensity — the base that supports everything
  - Zone 2 training: 150-180 min/week, conversational pace
  - How to train: walking fast, jogging slow, rucking, cycling, swimming
  - Why it matters: the single most protective factor against cardiovascular disease, metabolic disease, and cognitive decline

### Chapter 15: The IXNAH Conditioning Protocols
*Specific conditioning work for the capable human.*

- 15.1 — Sprint protocol ❌
  - 6-10 × 30m, 2x/week
  - Barefoot on grass (connects to Chapter 3)
  - Progressions: distance, incline, sled

- 15.2 — Heavy bag conditioning ⚠️
  - 3-6 rounds × 3 minutes, 1-minute rest
  - Combines striking practice with cardio
  - The most time-efficient conditioning for a martial artist

- 15.3 — Rucking ❌
  - Walk with 15-20kg backpack, 30-60 minutes, 1x/week
  - Aerobic base + leg durability + loaded carry
  - The most underrated longevity exercise

- 15.4 — Jump rope ⚠️
  - 10-15 minutes continuous
  - Develops rhythm, footwork, calf endurance
  - Connection to boxing footwork and judo mat movement

- 15.5 — Swimming as survival conditioning ❌
  - Not competitive swimming — survival swimming
  - Tread water 5 min, swim 200m, float 5 min, enter deep water
  - 4-6 sessions to develop basic water competency

  - Purpose: deep relaxation, stress reduction, restorative sleep
  - Mastery: enter deep relaxation in under 5 minutes

- 17.6 — Rayo Interior (Holotropic Breathing) ✅
  - 15-30 minutes rapid, deep mouth breathing
  - Purpose: altered consciousness, unlock repressed emotions
  - Mastery: sustain without losing control, enter deep perception states
  - Warning: not casual. This is advanced work.

- 17.7 — How to use the spells together ❌
  - Pre-training: Wim Hof → Warrior Mode
  - Post-training: Jaguar Pulse or Silent Wind → Mystic Mode
