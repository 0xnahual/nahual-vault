# IXNAH Domain Architecture — The 7 Domains

*7 domains. Everything the human body can do, fight, be, and become.*

---

## Structure

The 7 domains are organized in a natural arc:

```
WHAT THE BODY DOES:
  1 — MOVING           Locomotion, agility, controlled transitions
  2 — INNER ANIMAL     Primal capability, climbing, gripping, crawling, sprinting

HOW THE BODY FIGHTS:
  3 — DEFENDING        Combat, survival, awareness, neutralization

WHAT THE BODY IS:
  4 — STRENGTH         Peak force production, the 7 Compounds
  5 — ENDURANCE        How much it takes and keeps going

WHAT THE BODY BECOMES:
  6 — LONGEVITY        Capability sustained across decades
  7 — EXPRESSION       Joy, art, play, the voice, the creation
```

**DO → FIGHT → BE → BECOME.**

Each domain answers a different question:

| Domain | The Question |
|--------|-------------|
| [[1 — MOVING|MOVING]] | Can this body get there? |
| [[2 — INNER ANIMAL|INNER ANIMAL]] | Can this body survive in nature? |
| [[3 — DEFENDING|DEFENDING]] | Can this body protect itself and others? |
| [[4 — STRENGTH|STRENGTH]] | Can this body produce force? |
| [[7 — ENDURANCE|ENDURANCE]] | Can this body take punishment and keep going? |
| [[5 — LONGEVITY|LONGEVITY]] | Can this body do all of this at 70? |
| [[6 — EXPRESSION|EXPRESSION]] | Does this body WANT to do it again? |

## Domain 1 — MOVING

**Locomotion, agility, controlled transitions.**

The most fundamental domain. Before you can fight, lift, or endure — you must be able to move. MOVING is about controlled, efficient, adaptable locomotion — on any surface, in any direction, under any condition.

### What It Contains

- **The 7 Medicine Postures:** Ma Bu, Gong Bu, Pu Bu, Asian Squat, Dead Hang, Tree Pose, Forward Fold
- **The 4 Sacred Mobility Portals:** Hip (Gate of Power), Hamstring (Bowstring of the Warrior), Thoracic (Axis of Spirit), Ankle/Foot (Root of the Beast)
- **Stance Transitions:** Ma Bu → Gong Bu → Pu Bu → Xie Bu, flowing between positions
- **Silk Reeling & Internal Movement:** Hip-leading motion, spiral force, Qi Gong integration
- **Barefoot Practice:** Foot health, proprioception, natural gait
- **Ground-to-Standing Transitions:** Getting up and down fluidly

### What It Is NOT

MOVING is HUMAN movement — controlled, precise, efficient. It is not ANIMAL movement (that's [[2 — INNER ANIMAL|INNER ANIMAL]]). The distinction: MOVING is the stance transition done with perfect form. INNER ANIMAL is the scramble up a wall done with whatever works.

### Mastery Standards

- Ma Bu: 2 min at parallel, calm breathing
- Asian Squat: 5 min comfortable, flat heels
- Dead Hang: 60 sec passive, controlled breath
- Tree Pose: 60 sec each side, eyes closed
- Stance transitions: 5 min freestyle flow without losing structure
- Barefoot run: 2 km mixed terrain, no pain

### [[The 9 Pillars — The Architecture of Power|9 Pillars]] Connection

| Pillar | How MOVING Trains It |
|--------|---------------------|
| Presencia | Stance transitions demand presence — lose focus, lose form |
| Fluir | Silk reeling IS flow. The body synchronizes with itself. |
| Atención | Where the eyes go during movement. Where the weight shifts. |

## Domain 2 — INNER ANIMAL

**Primal capability. Back to monke. The animal body reclaimed from domestication.**

The human body was built for climbing, crawling, hanging, sprinting, gripping, jumping, swimming. Civilization removed all of it. INNER ANIMAL is the systematic reclamation of these capabilities — not through structured exercise, but through raw, unstructured, natural physical challenge.

This is not [[4 — STRENGTH|STRENGTH]] (which is structured, progressive, measured). This is the jungle. The gorilla doesn't do 5×5. The gorilla climbs, grips, moves, and is powerful because its body interacts with the ENVIRONMENT, not with equipment.

### What It Contains

- **Climbing:** Rope climb, wall climb, tree climb, rock scrambling. Vertical movement on irregular surfaces.
- **Brachiating:** Bar-to-bar swinging, monkey bars, hanging traverses. Moving THROUGH the air using grip.
- **Crawling:** Bear crawl, army crawl, crawling under obstacles. Ground locomotion on all fours.
- **Sprinting:** 30-60m predator sprint. Maximum speed. Chase or escape. Not cardio — survival speed.
- **Gorilla Grip:** Towel hangs, gi grips, irregular object holding, crushing force. The grip that doesn't let go.
- **Raw Hanging:** Long-duration hangs, one-arm hangs, rope hangs, branch-thickness bars. Living in the trees.
- **Dragging:** Sled pull, rope pull, dragging weight across ground. Moving heavy things without lifting.
- **Explosive Jumping:** Broad jump, precision jump, depth jump. Clearing gaps. Reaching heights.
- **Swimming:** Open water survival. Underwater breath holds. Aquatic movement.
- **Throwing Objects:** Stone put, medicine ball throw, sandbag toss. Projecting force into objects.
- **Environmental Interaction:** Moving through uncontrolled spaces — parks, forests, urban terrain.

### The Philosophy

An [[What is IXNAH|IXNAH]] warrior is not aggressive — he is a fucking animal who can neutralize you, but will not attack you. The INNER ANIMAL domain builds the raw physical capability. The [[3 — DEFENDING|DEFENDING]] domain decides when and how to use it. The animal is dangerous by nature, not by choice. Danger is a byproduct of capability, not a goal.

### How It Differs From Other Domains

| INNER ANIMAL | vs. | Other Domain |
|---|---|---|
| Climbing a wall | MOVING | Walking to the wall |
| Gorilla grip on irregular surface | STRENGTH | Pullup grip on a smooth bar |
| Predator sprint (survival speed) | ENDURANCE | Sustained running (cardio) |
| Crawling under obstacle | MOVING | Stance transition on flat ground |
| Broad jump (clear the gap) | STRENGTH | Box jump (plyometric training) |

**INNER ANIMAL is the jungle. [[4 — STRENGTH|STRENGTH]] is the gym. [[1 — MOVING|MOVING]] is the dojo. Same body, different context.**

### Mastery Standards

- Rope climb: Full rope, no legs (arms + grip only)
- Sprint: 40m in under 6 seconds
- Gorilla hang: 2 min dead hang on towel/irregular surface
- Broad jump: Standing broad jump > body height
- Crawl: 50m bear crawl without stopping
- Climb: Scale a 2m wall from standing start
- Swim: 200m open water, comfortable

### [[The 9 Pillars — The Architecture of Power|9 Pillars]] Connection

| Pillar | How INNER ANIMAL Trains It |
|--------|---------------------------|
| Energía | The animal knows when it has fuel and when it doesn't. Energy awareness becomes instinct. |
| Instinto (via Atención) | Reading the environment. Where to grip. Where to step. Where to jump. |
| Voluntad | The rope doesn't care about your feelings. Climb or fall. |

## Domain 3 — DEFENDING

**Combat application, survival, awareness, neutralization.**

The IXNAH warrior does not attack. All combat is defensive — neutralization, control, protection. The warrior is the most dangerous person in the room and the least likely to fight. The animal qualities ([[2 — INNER ANIMAL|INNER ANIMAL]]) make him capable. The defending philosophy makes him restrained. That is the Nahual.

### What It Contains

**Pre-Combat (Awareness & De-Escalation):**
- Reading environments — exits, threats, terrain
- Pre-fight indicators — reading body language, recognizing aggression patterns
- The Decision Tree: Avoid → De-escalate → Escape → Neutralize
- Verbal de-escalation techniques
- Strategic positioning (back to wall, near exit)

**Grappling Combat (Judo + Future BJJ):**
- **Kuzushi** — off-balancing (the foundation of all throwing)
- **Ashi-waza** — foot techniques (sweeps, trips, reaps)
- **Koshi-waza** — hip throws (the devastating throws on concrete)
- **Te-waza** — hand techniques (arm throws, drop techniques)
- **Combination throwing** — chaining failed techniques into successful ones
- **Clinch control** — grip fighting (kumi-kata), position dominance
- **Ne-waza** — ground work (pins, escapes, submissions [future BJJ])

**No Striking.** The IXNAH warrior does not punch or kick offensively. Striking is designed to DAMAGE. Judo is designed to CONTROL. A throw controls without necessarily hurting. A submission offers the opponent a choice — submit or be submitted. Striking removes the choice. IXNAH preserves it.

**Survival Skills:**
- **4 Breakfalls (Ukemi):** Mae ukemi (forward), Yoko ukemi (side), Ushiro ukemi (back), Rolling breakfall
- Ground-to-standing: Technical standup from any position
- Guard retention and escapes (shrimping, bridging, turtle)
- Multiple attacker awareness (circle movement, never get surrounded, throw hard into escape routes)
- Weapon threat protocol (compliance first — run if possible, close distance if not)

### Why No Striking

A punch to the head on concrete can kill. A throw on concrete is devastating but survivable. The IXNAH warrior's combat path is: close distance (INNER ANIMAL speed) → clinch (gorilla grip) → throw (Judo technique) → control (ground dominance) → walk away. At every stage, the warrior controls the level of force. The opponent is neutralized, not destroyed.

The gap between standing apart and clinch range — where punches live — is closed with explosive speed from the INNER ANIMAL domain, not with counter-striking. The gorilla doesn't box its way to the clinch. The gorilla ARRIVES at the clinch.

### Mastery Standards

- Breakfall: All 4 on hard surface
- Throw: 3+ different throws in live randori
- Ground: Escape mount and side control in live rolling
- Awareness: Describe exits and threats in new room within 10 seconds
- Clinch: Enter clinch from standing distance, control position for 10 seconds
- Decision tree: Demonstrated verbal de-escalation in role-play scenario

### [[The 9 Pillars — The Architecture of Power|9 Pillars]] Connection

| Pillar | How DEFENDING Trains It |
|--------|------------------------|
| Atención | Reading the opponent. Seeing the weight shift before the throw. Seeing the aggressor before the attack. |
| Balance | Knowing when to yield and when to resist. The throw uses the opponent's force — that's balance in combat. |
| Desapego | Being thrown in randori. Losing. Getting up. Not carrying the loss. The ego dies on the mat. |
| Entrega | Ukemi — surrendering to the fall. The body catches what the mind released. |

## Domain 4 — STRENGTH

**Peak force production. The [[IXNAH Strength — The 7 Compounds|7 Compounds]]. Progressive overload.**

STRENGTH is structured, measurable, progressive. Unlike [[2 — INNER ANIMAL|INNER ANIMAL]] (which is raw and environmental), STRENGTH follows a system: load the pattern, increase the load, master the movement, add more load. The gym. The program. The numbers.

### What It Contains

**The 7 Compounds (Full Movement Pattern Coverage):**

| # | Movement | Pattern | Mastery Test |
|---|----------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Pullup | Vertical Pull | 15 strict OR 5× at +20 kg |
| 2 | Body Row | Horizontal Pull | 10 feet-elevated with 2-sec pause |
| 3 | Dip | Vertical Push | 10 strict OR 5× at +15 kg |
| 4 | Pushup | Horizontal Push | 10 diamond OR 5 one-arm each side |
| 5 | Squat (loaded) | Knee Dominant | 5 × 5 with 40 kg sandbag, full depth |
| 6 | KB Swing | Hip Dominant | 10 × 10 at 32 kg in under 10 min |
| 7 | Farmer's Carry | Loaded Carry | 40m unbroken at 40 kg each hand |

**Grip — The Master Attribute:**

Grip limits actual strength. Everything the upper body does transmits force through the hands. Weak grip = leaked power. Strong grip = the chain holds. Grip is the #1 predictor of all-cause mortality and the #1 factor in Judo. It is woven into every compound through equipment variations (towel pullups, fat bar, heavy KB, gi grips).

**Full documentation:** See [[IXNAH Strength — The 7 Compounds]]

### How It Differs From INNER ANIMAL

| STRENGTH | INNER ANIMAL |
|----------|-------------|
| Pullup: 5 × 5 weighted, smooth bar | Climbing: irregular surface, arms + grip only |
| KB swing: programmed sets and reps | Throwing rocks: raw, unstructured power |
| Farmer's carry: measured distance, controlled | Dragging: uneven terrain, unknown weight |
| Progressive overload | Environmental challenge |

**STRENGTH is the gym. INNER ANIMAL is the jungle. Same muscles. Different intention.**

### [[The 9 Pillars — The Architecture of Power|9 Pillars]] Connection

## Domain 5 — ENDURANCE

**How much can you take — and keep going?**

ENDURANCE is the domain of sustained effort, impact absorption, and structural resilience. It is not [[4 — STRENGTH|STRENGTH]] (peak force output). It is not [[5 — LONGEVITY|LONGEVITY]] (decades-long sustainability). ENDURANCE is about RIGHT NOW: can your cardiovascular system sustain effort? Can your bones absorb impact? Can your tendons handle load? Can your body take punishment and not break?

### What It Contains

**Cardiovascular Conditioning ([[IXNAH Conditioning — The 3 Engines|The 3 Engines]]):**

| Engine | What It Powers | Duration | IXNAH Method |
|--------|---------------|----------|-------------|
| Alactic (ATP-CP) | Explosive burst, one throw, one sprint | 0-10 sec | Clap pushups, box jumps, short sprints with full rest |
| Glycolytic (Lactic) | Sustained fight effort, the round | 10 sec - 2 min | Judo randori, HIIT circuits, heavy bag (future), timed rounds |
| Aerobic (Oxidative) | Recovery between efforts, recovery between days | 2+ min | Zone 2 jogging, walking, easy jump rope, Qi Gong |

**The accounting rule:** Count combat sessions before adding conditioning. Judo randori IS glycolytic conditioning. Don't stack more on top.

**Full documentation:** See [[IXNAH Conditioning — The 3 Engines]]

**Impact Conditioning:**
- **Hard Qi Gong (Iron Body):** Progressive conditioning of the body to absorb impact. Gradual exposure to strikes, falls, and compression. The body adapts to being hit — the tissue toughens, the nervous system stops panicking, the pain threshold rises.
- **Judo Ukemi (Repeated Falling):** Being thrown hundreds of times per month conditions the body to absorb impact. The breakfall becomes reflexive. The fear of falling dissolves. This is endurance training disguised as technique practice.
- **Contact Conditioning:** Partner drills where controlled impact is the training stimulus. Light body shots, controlled throws on mats, gradually increasing intensity. The body learns: impact is survivable.

**Bone Density:**
- Loaded impact builds bone: Judo throws (landing creates skeletal load), jumping (ground reaction force), heavy carries (compressive load on the spine and legs), KB swings (dynamic loading)
- Bone density peaks in the 30s and declines after. Training it NOW is investing in structural integrity for decades.
- The irony: everything that builds bone density also builds ENDURANCE. These are not separate goals.

**Tendon & Connective Tissue Resilience:**

Tendons adapt slower than muscles (weeks vs days). They are the springs and cables of the body — they store elastic energy (plyometrics) and transfer force (isometrics). Training them requires:

| Type | Method | Examples | What It Builds |
|------|--------|---------|----------------|
| Isometric holds | Long-duration static positions | Ma Bu (2+ min), Dead Hang (2+ min), L-sit, Wall Sit | Tendon stiffness, connective tissue density |
| Explosive plyometrics | Rapid stretch-shortening cycle | Clap pushups, depth jumps, broad jumps, sprawls | Elastic energy storage, tendon springiness |
| Heavy slow loading | Slow eccentric movements | Slow negative pullups, slow squat descents | Tendon thickness, injury prevention |

**Two expressions, one category:** Isometrics build the CABLE (rigid, strong, load-bearing). Plyometrics build the SPRING (elastic, explosive, reactive). Both are tendon training. Both live in ENDURANCE.

**Breath Endurance:**
- CO2 tolerance tables (breath holds with increasing duration)
- The 6 Breathing Spells (Hechicería del Aliento) — each one trains a different aspect of breath control
- Wim Hof method — extreme breath work + cold exposure
- The bridge between physical endurance and mental endurance: when the breath is controlled, everything is controlled

**Environmental Hardening:**
- Cold exposure (cold showers, ice baths, Wim Hof protocol)
- Heat exposure (sauna, training in heat)
- Discomfort tolerance: operating under conditions that are not ideal — tired, cold, hungry, uncomfortable. The body learns: this is survivable.

### The Overlap With Combat

Judo is the single greatest ENDURANCE training in IXNAH. A randori session trains:
- Cardiovascular system (glycolytic)
- Impact conditioning (being thrown repeatedly)
- Bone density (landing forces)
- Grip endurance (gripping under fatigue)
- Mental endurance (continuing when exhausted)
- Pain tolerance (everything hurts by round 3)

Judo is combat training ([[3 — DEFENDING|DEFENDING]]) that produces endurance (ENDURANCE) as a byproduct. The domains overlap. This is correct — the body doesn't train one domain at a time.

### Mastery Standards

- Zone 2: 30 min sustained, conversational pace
- Sprint: 6 × 40m with full recovery, no speed drop-off
- Ma Bu hold: 3 minutes (tendon + mental endurance)
- Dead hang: 2 minutes (tendon + grip endurance)
- Judo randori: 5 × 3-minute rounds without fading
- Cold: 2-minute cold shower without panic breathing
- Breath: 2-minute breath hold after Wim Hof protocol
- Impact: All 4 breakfalls on hard surface, no hesitation

### [[The 9 Pillars — The Architecture of Power|9 Pillars]] Connection

| Pillar | How ENDURANCE Trains It |
|--------|------------------------|
| Voluntad | The hold at minute 2. The round at minute 3. The cold that doesn't stop. Will is endurance of the mind. |
| Energía | Knowing how much fuel is left. Pacing. Not spending everything in round 1. |
| Balance | Hard day, easy day. Push week, deload week. Training the body without breaking it. |
| Entrega | The moment in a long hold when you stop fighting the pain and just LET IT BE THERE. Surrender to discomfort without surrendering the position. |

## Domain 6 — LONGEVITY

**Capability sustained across decades. Not just living long — living CAPABLE.**

LONGEVITY is the only domain measured in decades, not months. Every other domain asks: can you do this NOW? LONGEVITY asks: can you do this at 60, 70, 80? The body that is strong but fragile breaks eventually. The body that is powerful but unsustainable burns out. LONGEVITY is the domain that ensures everything else LASTS.

### What It Contains

**Structural Maintenance:**
- The 7 Medicine Postures (daily minimum)
- The 4 Sacred Mobility Portals (3x/week minimum)
- Joint health: CARs (Controlled Articular Rotations), progressive loading within safe ranges
- Barefoot practice (foot health and proprioception decline fastest)
- Spinal health: dead hangs (decompression), Jefferson curls (segmental articulation), back bridges (extension)

**[[The 6 Master Flexibility Positions]] (Long-Term Goals):**
Dead Hang, Back Bridge, Middle Split, Front Split (L), Front Split (R), Pancake Fold. These ensure the body maintains RANGE across decades, not just strength.

**Nervous System Mastery:**
- Warrior Mode (sympathetic activation — on demand)
- Mystic Mode (parasympathetic activation — on demand)
- The Switch: choosing your state at will, not being hijacked by stress
- The 6 Breathing Spells:
  1. Pulso del Jaguar (Box Breathing) — calm craving, reduce stress
  2. Hechizo de Wim Hof — extreme energy, cold resistance
  3. Viento Silencioso (4-7-8) — calm mind, sleep
  4. Tiempo en Equilibrio (Square) — nervous system balance
  5. Luna Silenciosa (Left Nostril) — deep relaxation
  6. Rayo Interior (Holotropic) — altered consciousness, emotional release

**Zone 2 Cardio (The Longevity Engine):**
150-180 min/week of conversational-pace cardio. THE most evidence-backed longevity intervention. It exists in ENDURANCE as a training modality and in LONGEVITY as a survival strategy. Train it for endurance. Keep it for longevity.

**Fall Prevention:**
The 4 Breakfalls (from [[3 — DEFENDING|DEFENDING]]) are the #1 longevity skill from martial arts. Falls are the leading cause of injury death in the elderly. A trained breakfall turns a hip-breaking fall into a controlled landing. Every Judo session is longevity training.

**Chen Tai Chi (Future):**
The longevity martial art. Trainable at 60, 70, 80. No impact. No partner required. Balance, proprioception, breathing, calm. The art the Nahual practices when the body can no longer be thrown.

**Grip Maintenance:**
Grip strength declines fastest with age. Dead hang holds, towel hangs, farmer's carries — grip training is longevity training. The research is clear: grip strength is the single strongest predictor of all-cause mortality.

### The Longevity Test

Can you do this at 70?

| Test | Standard |
|------|---------|
| Zone 2 | 30 min sustained, conversational pace |
| Grip | 90 sec dead hang |
| Balance | Tree Pose 60 sec, eyes closed |
| Squat | Asian squat 5 min, heels flat |
| Breakfall | All 4 on hard surface |
| Breathing | Pulso del Jaguar → calm in 60 sec |
| Walk | 5 km continuous, no pain |
| Stand from floor | Ground to standing without using hands |

### [[The 9 Pillars — The Architecture of Power|9 Pillars]] Connection

| Pillar | How LONGEVITY Trains It |
|--------|------------------------|
| Desapego | Releasing attachment to peak performance. The body changes. Detach from the numbers. |
| Entrega | Aging while maintaining capability. Surrendering youth without surrendering power. |
| Presencia | Being fully in the body as it ages. Not living in memories of past performance. |
| Balance | The oscillation between training hard enough to grow and resting enough to last. |

## Domain 7 — EXPRESSION

**Joy as a training domain. The pressure release valve. The art.**

Every other domain answers: what can this body DO? EXPRESSION answers: what does this body WANT to do?

A human who can fight, lift, run, and endure but cannot PLAY is a machine. Without EXPRESSION, IXNAH becomes another optimization system. And optimization systems have a 95% dropout rate because they kill the joy.

EXPRESSION is architecturally necessary. It keeps the practice alive across decades because it's the only domain that doesn't require discipline — you do it because it feels good.

### What It Contains

**Movement Play:**
- Kick exploration: spinning kicks, high kicks, flashy combinations. Not combat-effective. Not practical. FUN.
- Flow movement: improvised movement, transitions between stances and rolls
- Dance-adjacent movement: moving to music, shadow boxing as dance

**The Voice:**
- Vocal training (daily practice, weekly coach)
- Singing as physical expression — the body IS the instrument
- Performance: the voice + the body + the movement = the Nahual's art

**Music:**
- NΛHUΛL: raw, acoustic, Spanish, folk. The human voice carrying truth.
- Ixtlan Project: electronic, experimental, the inner world made audible.
- Multi-instrumental practice: guitar, keys/synth, percussion, voice.

**The Writing:**
- The book. The transformation narrative. Toltec philosophy through lived experience.
- Content creation: morning practice recordings, movement videos, philosophical fragments.

**The Code:**
- El Templo: the app that makes IXNAH accessible. Technology as creative expression.

**Martial Arts Aesthetics:**
- Forms/kata (moving meditation, physical poetry)
- Weapons play (future — staff, sword, for beauty, not combat)

### The Rule

**EXPRESSION is not tracked.** Not gamified. Not streaked. Not mastery-tested. The moment you add a progress bar to joy, you've killed it. Expression lives OUTSIDE El Templo. It is the domain that the system protects by leaving it alone.

### [[The 9 Pillars — The Architecture of Power|9 Pillars]] Connection

| Pillar | How EXPRESSION Trains It |
|--------|-------------------------|
| Fluir | Flow is where expression lives. The moment structure disappears and movement becomes play. |
| Entrega | Performance IS surrender. The singer who has prepared everything and then lets go. The practice doing itself. |
| Presencia | Creation happens now or not at all. Expression demands presence because art cannot be produced by a mind that's elsewhere. |

## How the 7 Domains Interact

No domain exists in isolation. Every training session, every movement, every practice touches multiple domains simultaneously.

```
JUDO RANDORI touches:
  DEFENDING    — combat technique, neutralization
  ENDURANCE    — cardiovascular, impact conditioning, tendon loading
  INNER ANIMAL — gorilla grip, explosive clinch entry
  STRENGTH     — pulling power, isometric grip
  LONGEVITY    — breakfalls, bone density
  MOVING       — balance, footwork, weight shifting

MA BU HOLD touches:
  MOVING       — stance mastery, alignment
  STRENGTH     — quad and core loading
  ENDURANCE    — tendon resilience, mental endurance, pain tolerance
  LONGEVITY    — joint health, pelvic alignment
```

**The domains are LENSES, not silos.** The same exercise is seen differently through each domain's lens. The pullup in STRENGTH is about progressive overload. The pullup in INNER ANIMAL is about climbing a wall. The pullup's contribution to ENDURANCE is grip duration. Its contribution to LONGEVITY is grip strength maintenance across decades.
