## Why This Schedule

- **Monday Judo dropped** — they do heavy strength work, which is redundant with IXNAH morning training. You control your own strength programming better.
- **Wednesday + Friday Judo kept** — technique sessions. Throws, entries, transitions, ground work, randori. This is what requires a partner and coach. Irreplaceable.
- **K1 on Tuesday + Thursday** — alternates with Judo. Striking one day, grappling the next. Different stress types = better recovery.
- **Monday morning = Power day** — no evening session means you can go hard. Fresh body, max effort. Power output feeds directly into the martial arts sessions for the rest of the week.

## Monday — IXNAH Power Day

### Purpose
Train the nervous system to fire fast. Upper and lower body explosive power. Directly transfers to Judo throws and K1 striking.

### Structure
Short and intense. 20-30 minutes. Full recovery between sets. Max effort each rep. This is NOT conditioning — this is power.

### Movements

| Movement | Sets × Reps | Purpose | IXNAH Domain |
|----------|-------------|---------|--------------|
| Clap / Explosive Pushups | 4 × 5-8 | Upper body power, punch speed | Strength + Violence |
| Box Jumps or Tuck Jumps | 4 × 5 | Lower body power, kick explosiveness | Moving + Violence |
| Kettlebell Swings (explosive) | 4 × 10 | Hip power — direct transfer to Judo throws and K1 kicks | Strength |
| Broad Jumps | 3 × 5 | Horizontal power, entry/exit speed | Violence + Defending |
| Sprawls | 4 × 6-8 | Defensive explosiveness, anti-takedown | Defending |

### Why Sprawls Matter
The most underrated exercise for a martial artist doing both striking and grappling:
- **K1:** Explosive hip drop to avoid clinch attempts, return to stance
- **Judo:** Anti-takedown defense, scramble recovery
- **IXNAH:** Bridges the Violence and Defending domains — the same movement pattern serves both attack recovery and defense

### Programming Notes
- Warm up with silk reeling + stance transitions (5-10 min) — the internal work before the external explosion
- Full rest between sets (60-90 seconds minimum). Power ≠ cardio.
- Progress by increasing height (box jumps), adding weight (KB swings), or reducing ground contact time (pushups, jumps)
- If CNS feels fried by Thursday, reduce Monday volume. Power day should ENHANCE the week, not drain it.

## Recovery Protocol

- **Sleep:** 8 hours. Non-negotiable. Already at 100%.
- **Nutrition:** Follow Karen's plan DAILY. 2,800 kcal. 189g protein. Tell her about K1 addition for potential calorie adjustment.
- **Water:** 2.5 liters/day (Karen's recommendation).
- **Supplements:** Proteína, Creatina, Omega 3, Magnesio, Probióticos.
- **Electrolytes:** 500ml once per week (Karen's recommendation).
- **Monday evening:** Dedicated recovery. Stretch, foam roll, or simply rest. The body adapts during rest, not during training.

## Trigger System (from Training & Phase Analysis)

### Monthly Check (Last Sunday of Month)
- Are PRs still progressing? (Check El Templo app)
- Are pliegues moving down? (Every 4-6 weeks with Karen)
- Am I hitting creative blocks? (Check "Touched a Project" habit)
- Is food logging at 70%+? (Check Daily Macros count)
- How is work performance?

### Adjustment Triggers

| If This Happens | Then |
|----------------|------|
| PRs stall for 3+ weeks | Drop K1 to 1x/week. Recovery is the bottleneck. |
| Pliegues go wrong direction | Nutrition is the problem. Focus on logging before anything else. |
| Creative blocks missed 2+ weeks | Drop K1 to 1x/week. Creative output is the actual goal. |
| Injury from any martial art | Drop ALL martial arts until healed. Morning IXNAH continues. |
| Food logging below 50% for a month | Drop to 1x K1 until logging hits 70%. |
| Work performance drops | Martial arts volume is the first cut. Career funds everything. |
| Monday power day making you fatigued by Thursday | Reduce Monday volume or switch to lighter plyometrics. |

**The rule: martial arts volume is earned through nutrition discipline and creative output.**

## The Logic

```
Monday AM: POWER (explosive) → feeds Tuesday-Friday martial arts
Monday PM: OFF → recovery + optional creative time
Tuesday:   PULL + K1 → upper body strength + striking
Wednesday: MOBILITY + JUDO → recovery morning + technique evening
Thursday:  LEGS + K1 → lower body strength + striking
Friday:    MOBILITY + JUDO → recovery morning + technique evening
Saturday:  CREATE → the whole point of La Forja
Sunday:    PUSH + RUN → upper body + cardio, close the week
```

Internal practice every morning. External combat every evening (except Monday and weekends). Power on Monday. Technique on Wednesday/Friday. Striking on Tuesday/Thursday. Create on Saturday. The week has rhythm.
