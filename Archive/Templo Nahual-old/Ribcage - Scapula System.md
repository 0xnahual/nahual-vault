# 🟩 RIBCAGE / SCAPULA SYSTEM (NAHUAL MODE)

## 1. Back-Body Breathing
- Ribs expand sideways and backwards.
- Chest does NOT rise.
- Diaphragm descends fully.
- Lower traps + serratus stabilize the ribcage.
- Breath stays in the “water bottle shape.”

**Cues**
- “Expand without deforming.”
- “Float the ribs open.”
- “Exhale without collapsing.”

---

## 2. Scapular Control
- Scapula depress (down) before any pull.
- Serratus wraps ribs and controls protraction.
- Lower traps handle depression and stabilization.
- Upper traps stay quiet.

**Cues**
- “Shoulders in your pockets.”
- “Slide the blades down your spine.”
- “Move the scapula, not the arms.”

---

## 3. Dead Hang Integration
- Full relax to decompress spine.
- Grip activates forearm–lat connection.
- Ribcage opens under gravity.
- Nervous system learns to stay calm under load.

**Daily Target**
- 2 minutes total hang time (broken up as needed).

---

## 4. Scapular Shrug Pattern
- Start in dead hang.
- Pull ONLY with scapula (no elbows).
- Depression first, then slight retraction.
- Slow negative to engrave the circuit.

**Daily Target**
- 3–6 sets of 5–8 controlled reps.

---

## 5. Hanging Core (Leg/Knee Raises)
- Abs, obliques, serratus create a 360° brace.
- Hip flexors strengthen without compensations.
- Grip endurance improves automatically.
- Scapular control maintained during movement.

**Execution**
- Zero swing.
- Slow up, slower down.
- Knees → Legs progression.
