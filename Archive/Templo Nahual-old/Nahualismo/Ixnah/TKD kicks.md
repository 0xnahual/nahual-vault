### **1.** 

### **Front Push Kick (Teep / Ap Chagi)**

- **Function**: Distance control, “jab of the legs.”
    
- **Why**: Sets up judo entries by making people stumble back → you step in to throw.
    
- **Nahual vibe**: Looks simple, but when you deliver it with crisp form it’s like a sudden lightning strike.
    

---

### **2.** 

### **Roundhouse Kick (Dollyo Chagi)**

- **Function**: Bread-and-butter strike. Works low (leg), mid (ribs), high (head).
    
- **Why**: Universal, fast, combos perfectly with hands or throws.
    
- **Nahual vibe**: Clean arc, can be explosive or flowing. This is your “anime frame” kick.
    

---

### **3.** 

### **Side Kick (Yeop Chagi)**

- **Function**: Long-range power shot; best for stopping forward pressure.
    
- **Why**: In self-defense or randori-style chaos, it creates massive space.
    
- **Nahual vibe**: Feels like a spear thrust — precise, penetrating, dramatic.
    

---

### **4.** 

### **Spinning Back Kick (Dwi Chagi)**

- **Function**: Counter weapon — punishes people who rush in.
    
- **Why**: Very practical if timed well, devastating if landed.
    
- **Nahual vibe**: The cinematic “whiplash” strike, perfect for reels.
    

---

### **5.** 

### **Axe Kick (Naeryo Chagi)**

- **Function**: Overhead smash. Not common in MMA, but iconic in TKD.
    
- **Why**: Flashy, demoralizing, and can be disguised from chamber of roundhouse/side kick.
    
- **Nahual vibe**: Dropping the sky on someone’s head = pure archetype.
    

---

### **(Optional Bonus)** 

### **Spinning Hook Kick (Dwi Huryeo Chagi)**

- **Function**: KO highlight-reel move.
    
- **Why**: Less practical, but legendary aesthetic.
    
- **Nahual vibe**: Anime final boss finisher. Train it, but don’t rely on it.
