2. **You DROP Friday Judo.** So you go from 3 double days to 4, but one of the old double days (Friday) becomes single. Net increase: only 1 more session total.
3. **Creative time isn't zero.** You lose Tue/Thu evenings but Friday evening opens up. And weekends exist.
4. **The recomp concern is real but solvable.** If you're aware you need to eat more to compensate for the extra sessions, and you actually log your food, the recomp continues. The +2.9 kg muscle happened at current volume — with slightly more volume, you might gain slightly less (say +2.0-2.5 kg) but you'd still be gaining.

**The risk:** It only works if you actually show up for weekend creative blocks AND increase food intake to compensate. If you don't eat enough, the recomp stalls. If you don't protect weekend creative time, the SHIP objectives die.

## Part 3: My Actual Recommendation (Revised)

**Option C: 2x K1, drop Friday Judo.**

I changed my mind. Here's why:

1. **You clearly want this.** Motivation matters. Forcing yourself to skip something you're pulled toward creates resentment. K1 feeds your energy.
2. **IXNAH needs it.** A martial arts system without live striking training is incomplete. 2x K1 per week gives you real credentials in the Violence and Expression domains.
3. **The math works.** 11 sessions instead of 10. Only 1 more than current. The alternating stress types (grappling → striking → grappling → striking) are actually smart for recovery.
4. **Creative time shrinks but doesn't die.** You lose 2 weeknight evenings but gain 1 (Friday opens up). Net loss: 1 evening. Weekend creative blocks fill the gap — IF you protect them.

**Two non-negotiable conditions:**

1. **Food logging goes to 70%+ of days.** At 40%, you can't manage the recomp with higher volume. You'll accidentally under-eat and stall. This is the price of admission for K1.
2. **Saturday creative block is protected like training.** Saturday 10 AM - 1 PM (or whatever time): music production, app development, book writing. Non-negotiable. Not "I'll work on stuff." A scheduled block with a specific task. Written in the weekly template.

If you can commit to those two things, Option C is the move.

## Part 6: The Trigger System

Don't set this schedule in stone forever. Build in checkpoints.

### Monthly Check (During Weekly Review, Last Sunday of Month)
- Are PRs still progressing? (Check El Templo app)
- Are pliegues moving in the right direction? (Every 4-6 weeks)
- Am I hitting both creative blocks per week? (Check "Touched a Project" habit)
- Is food logging at 70%+? (Check Daily Macros count)
- How is work performance? (Self-assess: energy levels, output quality)

### Adjustment Triggers

| If This Happens | Then |
|----------------|------|
| PRs stall for 3+ weeks | Drop K1 to 1x/week. Recovery is the bottleneck. |
| Pliegues go wrong direction | Nutrition is the problem. Focus on logging before anything else. |
| Creative blocks missed 2+ weeks in a row | Drop K1 to 1x/week. Creative output is the actual goal of La Forja. |
| Injury from any martial art | Drop ALL martial arts until healed. Morning training continues. Non-negotiable. |
| Food logging below 50% for a month | You haven't earned the K1 volume. Drop to Option B (1x K1) until logging hits 70%. |
| Work performance drops | Something has to give. Martial arts volume is the first cut. Career funds everything. |

**The rule: martial arts volume is earned through nutrition discipline and creative output.** If you're not logging food and not showing up for creative blocks, K1 is a distraction, not a tool. Earn it by being disciplined in the areas that actually move La Forja forward.
