## **Semester 1: The Foundation of the Pillar**

### **Month 1: Zhan Zhuang (The Art of Standing)**

_Focus: Erasing the ego's tension and building the "Internal Frame."_

Your objective is to stand perfectly still for **30 minutes daily**.

- **The Wuji Stance:** Feet parallel, shoulder-width apart. Weight centered on the bubbling well (the ball of the foot).
    
- **The Suspension:** Imagine a thread pulling the crown of your head ($Baihui$) up. Your chin is slightly tucked. This opens the cervical vertebrae.
    
- **The Sink:** Drop your tailbone as if sitting on a high stool. Do not arch your back—this is "tucking the _Mingmen_."
    
- **The Hug:** Hold your arms in a circle at chest height, palms facing you. Elbows must stay lower than the wrists.
    
- **The Internal Work:** Every time your mind wanders to your "achievements" or "goals," return to the breath. If your legs burn, sink lower. The burn is where the "Old Identity" dies.
    

### **Month 2: Chansigong (Silk Reeling Engine)**

_Focus: Training the muscles to move in spirals, not straight lines._

You cannot master Tai Chi without **Silk Reeling**. It is the "Engine" of the Chen style.

- **Dantian Rotation:** Hands on your lower belly. Practice rotating the pelvis in small, internal figure-eights. The movement must be tiny and deep.
    
- **Frontal Silk Reeling:** One arm moves in a circle.
    
    - **The Detail:** As the hand moves away, it spirals out ($Shun$ $Chan$). As it returns, it spirals in ($Ni$ $Chan$).
        
    - **The Rule:** The hand does not move the arm. The **Waist** moves the hand. If the waist stops, the hand stops.
        
- **Metric:** 100 reps per arm, every day. If your shoulder gets tired, you are using the wrong muscle. Use the core.
    

### **Month 3: The Kua & The Cat Walk**

_Focus: Rooted locomotion. Moving without "falling."_

- **Opening the Kua:** The _Kua_ is the inguinal fold (crease of the hip). You must learn to "fold" here to keep your upper body upright while the legs work.
    
- The Cat Walk: 1. Shift 100% of your weight to one leg.
    
    2. Step out with the other leg, touching only the heel. Zero weight. 3. Slowly shift the weight forward like pouring water from one glass to another.
    
    3. Your head height must not change. If you bob up and down, you have no root.
    
- **Metric:** Walk across your training space for 20 minutes. If you lose balance once, the set is 0. Start over.
